## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR 52.6 & 52.7)

## Currency Warning
Permission is required for almost all appeals. The test is "real prospect of success" or "some other compelling reason."

## Template Type
Interlocutory Application (Appellate)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Application:** Request for permission to appeal (PTA).
2. **Background:** Brief history of the proceedings.
3. **Threshold Test:**
   - Argument on "Real Prospect of Success."
   - Argument on "Compelling Reason" (if applicable, e.g., conflicting authorities).
4. **Second Appeals:** (If applicable) Raise an "important point of principle or practice."
5. **Oral Hearing Request:** State if an oral hearing is requested if PTA is refused on the papers.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Tanfern Ltd v Cameron-MacDonald:** Cited for the "next level" hierarchy and the meaning of "real prospect."
- **Promptness:** Must be made to the lower court first, then to the appeal court if refused.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The appellant seeks permission to appeal on the basis that the appeal has a real prospect of success, specifically regarding the interpretation of [Section X] which is a matter of significant legal uncertainty."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "gatekeeper" step. Small firms must manage client expectations here; statistically, many PTA applications are refused on the papers.
