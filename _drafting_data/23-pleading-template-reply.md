Court Rule (CPR 15.8)

## Currency Warning
A Reply is optional. If no Reply is filed, the Claimant is not taken to admit the facts stated in the Defence.

## Template Type
Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading:** Court, Claim Number, Parties.
2. **Introduction:** "1. Save in so far as it consists of admissions, the Claimant joins issue with the Defendant on its Defence."
3. **Response to Specific Allegations:** Deal with new facts or legal arguments raised in the Defence that were not covered in the Particulars of Claim.
4. **Conclusion:** "AND the Claimant repeats its claim for the relief sought in the Particulars of Claim."
5. **Statement of Truth.**

## Drafting Conventions
- **Do Not Repeat:** A Reply should not merely repeat what was in the Particulars of Claim.
- **Counter-Counterclaim:** If the Defendant filed a Counterclaim, the Claimant must file a "Defence to Counterclaim" (often combined into one document: "Reply and Defence to Counterclaim").

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The Claimant joins issue with the Defendant on its Defence. In response to paragraph 4 of the Defence, the Claimant denies that the goods were defective as alleged or at all."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to respond to specific factual assertions in a Defence that require a direct rebuttal before trial.
