## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR Part 47 / Practice Direction 47)

## Currency Warning
The "Electronic Bill of Costs" (Precedent S) is now mandatory for almost all multi-track civil cases where an order for costs is made after 6 April 2018.

## Template Type
Costs / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Precedent S (Electronic Bill):** An Excel spreadsheet with multiple tabs (Summary, Detail, Phases).
2. **Traditional 6-Column Bill (if applicable):**
   - Column 1: Profit Costs Claimed.
   - Column 2: VAT on Profit Costs.
   - Column 3: Disbursements Claimed.
   - Columns 4-6: (Left blank for the Costs Judge to fill in what is "Allowed").
3. **Certification:** Signed by the solicitor to certify the accuracy of the bill and compliance with the "indemnity principle."

## Drafting Conventions
- **Indemnity Principle:** The solicitor cannot claim more from the opponent than they have charged their own client.
- **Narrative:** A short summary of the work done during each phase should be included.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"I certify that this bill does not exceed the amount which my client is liable to pay me in respect of the work complained of."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The formal document required to recover legal costs from the losing party following a successful court case.
