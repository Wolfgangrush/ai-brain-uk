## Authority Tier
Statutory Requirement (Employment Rights Act 1996 s.203(3))

## Currency Warning
Generic "full and final settlement" clauses are often insufficient for statutory claims. The agreement must list the specific statutory claims being waived (e.g., unfair dismissal, discrimination).

## Template Type
Settlement / Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Employer and Employee.
2. **Termination Date:** Date the employment ends.
3. **Payments:**
   - Contractual payments (Notice pay, holiday pay).
   - Ex gratia payment (Settlement sum - often first £30,000 is tax-free).
4. **Waiver of Claims:** Exhaustive list of statutory and contractual claims being waived.
5. **Legal Advice:** Mandatory clause identifying the independent adviser.
6. **Statutory Conditions:** Statement that s.203 ERA 1996 conditions are met.
7. **Confidentiality & Non-Derogation:** No disclosure of terms; no disparaging comments.
8. **References:** Agreed wording for a future employment reference (Schedule 1).
9. **Costs:** Employer's contribution towards the employee's legal fees.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Independent Advice:** The agreement is void as a waiver of statutory rights unless the employee has received independent legal advice.
- **Tax Indemnity:** Ensure the employee indemnifies the employer for any tax/NI due on the ex gratia payment.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"This agreement is intended to satisfy the conditions for a settlement agreement as set out in section 203(3) of the Employment Rights Act 1996 and the equivalent provisions in other relevant statutes."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to finalize the termination of an employment relationship in a way that protects the employer from future tribunal claims.
