## Authority Tier
Administrative Procedure (CPR Part 83)

## Currency Warning
Only for County Court judgments over £600 (not including court fees). Cannot be used for debts under the Consumer Credit Act unless the debt is over £25,000.

## Template Type
Enforcement Instructions

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Transfer Up:** Using Form N293A to transfer the judgment from the County Court to the High Court for enforcement.
2. **Instructions:** Provide the HCEO (High Court Enforcement Officer) with the debtor's address and any known assets (e.g., vehicles).
3. **Writ of Control:** Requesting the court issue the writ.
4. **Fees:** Payment of the court fee and the HCEO's compliance fee.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Speed:** HCEOs are generally faster and more effective than County Court Bailiffs.
- **Accuracy:** Ensure the debtor's name matches the judgment exactly.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"I certify that the judgment or order remains unsatisfied... I request that the judgment be transferred to the High Court for enforcement by a Writ of Control."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The standard "effective" enforcement method for mid-range debts. High-pressure enforcement that often results in payment or a meaningful installment plan.
