## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (CPR Part 52)

## Currency Warning
Appellants must file the notice within 21 days of the lower court's decision unless a different time limit was set. Permission to appeal is usually required.

## Template Type
Appellate Notice

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Details of Claim:** Lower court claim number, names of parties.
2. **The Decision:** Date and name of the judge/court whose decision is being appealed.
3. **Permission to Appeal:** State whether permission was granted by the lower court or is being sought from the appeal court.
4. **Other Information:** Is the appellant in receipt of legal aid? Is the appeal urgent?
5. **Grounds of Appeal:** (Either in the form or as an attached document).
6. **Skeleton Argument:** (Either attached or to follow within 14 days of filing).
7. **Service Details:** Details of the respondent(s).
8. **Statement of Truth:** Standard CPR-compliant signature.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Brevity:** Do not include arguments in the grounds of appeal.
- **Form N161:** Used for all appeals except small claims (which use N164) and appeals to the Supreme Court.
- **Bundles:** Must be filed alongside the notice in accordance with PD 52B/C.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The appellant appeals against the decision of [Judge Name] dated [Date] on the grounds set out in the attached document."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary vehicle for initiating an appeal. Failure to file within the 21-day window is usually fatal without an extension of time application (which is rarely granted without exceptional reasons).
