Court Rule (CPR Part 22 & Practice Direction 22)

## Currency Warning
Wording was updated in April 2020 to include the warning about contempt of court. Old "short-form" truth statements are now non-compliant.

## Template Type
Style Guide / Mandatory Wording

## Structural Skeleton
**For a Statement of Case (Claim, Defence, PoC):**
"I believe [the Claimant believes] [the Defendant believes] that the facts stated in these [particulars of claim / defence / etc.] are true. I understand [the Claimant understands] that proceedings for contempt of court may be brought against anyone who makes, or causes to be made, a false statement in a document verified by a statement of truth without an honest belief in its truth."

**For a Witness Statement:**
"I believe that the facts stated in this witness statement are true. I understand that proceedings for contempt of court may be brought against anyone who makes, or causes to be made, a false statement in a document verified by a statement of truth without an honest belief in its truth."

## Drafting Conventions
- **Signatory:** Must be signed by the party personally or their legal representative.
- **Legal Representative Signing:** The representative must have the party's authority and must explain the consequences of a false statement to the client before signing.
- **Date:** Must be dated at the time of signing.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for every formal document filed in court. Failure to include a Statement of Truth can result in the document being struck out.
