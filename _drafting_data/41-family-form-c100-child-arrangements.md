## Authority Tier
Tier 3: Mandatory HMCTS Form (Children Act 1989 / FPR Part 12)

## Currency Warning
This form was significantly updated in 2024-2026 to emphasize non-court dispute resolution (NCDR). Version 02.26 is the current standard.

## Template Type
Child Arrangements Order Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Nature of Application:** Section 8 orders sought (Live with, Spend time with, Prohibited Steps, Specific Issue).
2. **The Children:** Full names, DOB, gender, and current living arrangements.
3. **The Respondents:** Details of parents or those with parental responsibility.
4. **MIAM Section:** Evidence of mediation attendance or exemption (Form FM1 equivalent).
5. **Safety and Protection:** Declaration of any risk of harm, domestic abuse, or social services involvement (cross-ref Form C1A).
6. **Reasons for Application:** Concise summary of why the order is in the child's best interests.
7. **Attending the Court:** Requirements for interpreters, disability access, or special measures.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Welfare Checklist:** Draft the "Reasons" section with the Section 1(3) Children Act 1989 factors in mind (the "Welfare Checklist").
- **Conciseness:** Avoid lengthy inflammatory narratives in the application form; save detailed evidence for the Witness Statement.
- **NCDR:** New rules require parties to explain why NCDR (e.g., mediation, arbitration) was not used.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The court’s main consideration is the child’s welfare... I apply for an order that the child(ren) [spend time with/live with] [Name]..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Primary tool for initiating private law children proceedings. Solo advocates should use the online "Apply to court about child arrangements" service for faster processing and automatic MIAM validation.
