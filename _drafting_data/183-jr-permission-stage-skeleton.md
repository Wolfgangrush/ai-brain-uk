## Currency Warning
Permission skeletons should be short (typically <20 pages). Focus on "arguability."

## Template Type
Judicial Review — Permission Stage Skeleton

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction**: Purpose of the hearing (if oral renewal) or paper application.
2. **The Arguability Test**: Why the grounds have a "realistic prospect of success."
3. **Key Points of Law**: Brief summary of the legal errors.
4. **Delay/Standing**: Addressing any procedural hurdles.
5. **Conclusion**: Request for permission to proceed to full hearing.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Focus**: This is not the full hearing; do not over-argue the merits.
- **Response to AoS**: Address the Defendant's summary grounds directly.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The purpose of the permission stage is to filter out claims which are hopeless or unarguable."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Vital for navigating the "filter" stage of JR. Often used in oral renewal hearings where brevity is key.
