## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR 40.6 / Consent Orders) / Common Law Practice

## Currency Warning
Ensure the Schedule remains confidential if intended; the Order itself is a matter of public record, but the Schedule may be kept private between the parties unless filed.

## Template Type
Consent Order / Settlement

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading:** Court, Claim Number, Parties.
2. **The Order:**
   - "BY CONSENT it is ordered that:"
   - "1. All further proceedings in this claim be stayed upon the terms set out in the [Schedule to this Order / Settlement Agreement dated [Date]], except for the purpose of enforcing those terms."
   - "2. Each party shall have permission to apply to the Court to enforce the terms [of the Schedule] without the need to bring a new claim."
   - "3. [Costs provision - e.g., 'There be no order as to costs' or 'The Defendant pay the Claimant's costs']."
3. **Signatures:** Signed by solicitors for both parties or by the parties themselves.

## Drafting Conventions
- **"Tomlin" Structure:** The stay is in the body of the order; the commercial terms are in the Schedule. This keeps the settlement terms (e.g., payment amounts) out of the public domain.
- **Liberty to Apply:** Critical to allow enforcement within the same proceedings rather than starting a new breach-of-contract claim.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"AND IT IS ORDERED THAT each party shall have liberty to apply to the court to enforce the terms of the settlement as set out in the Schedule to this order without the need to bring a fresh claim."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The standard "safe" way to settle a UK civil claim where payment is deferred or terms are complex/confidential.
