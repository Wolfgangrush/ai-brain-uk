## Authority Tier
Tier 1: Statutory Requirement (CPIA 1996 s6A)

## Currency Warning
Mandatory for all Crown Court cases. Failure to serve or providing an inconsistent statement can lead to adverse inferences under Section 11 CPIA 1996.

## Template Type
Defence Statement

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** Court, Case Number, Defendant, Charges.
2. **Nature of Defence:** Summary of the "positive" case (e.g., self-defence, alibi).
3. **Matters in Issue:** Specific facts from the Prosecution case that are disputed and why.
4. **Particulars of Fact:** Facts the Defendant intends to rely on.
5. **Points of Law:** Any legal arguments or authorities (e.g., s78 PACE).
6. **Alibi Details:** Names, DOBs, and addresses of alibi witnesses (if applicable).
7. **Disclosure Requests:** Requests for specific "unused material".
8. **Signature:** Ideally signed by the Defendant to confirm adoption of the contents.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Comprehensive but Concise:** Must cover all intended defences; if a defence is raised at trial that wasn't in the statement, the jury can be told to draw an inference.
- **Alibi Strictness:** Alibi details must be precise; "somewhere in London" is insufficient.
- **Legal Arguments:** Identify statutory or case law early to avoid being barred from raising them later.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The nature of the defence is self-defence. The Defendant takes issue with the allegation that he struck the first blow. The Defendant relies on the fact that the complainant was brandishing a bottle."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary tool for forcing the Prosecution to disclose "unused material" that might help the defence. For a small firm, a well-drafted statement is the best protection against late-stage prosecution surprises.
