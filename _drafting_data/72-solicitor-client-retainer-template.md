## Currency Warning
The 2014 Law Society Model CFA is the most recent "standard" but should be updated to reflect the *Belsner* judgment regarding informed consent and the *CFA Regulations 2013*.

## Template Type
Contract / Client Care

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Solicitor and Client.
2. **The Work Covered:** Specific scope of legal services.
3. **The "Win" Defined:** (e.g., "Settlement or judgment in your favour").
4. **Our Fees if You Win:**
   - Normal Hourly Rates.
   - Success Fee (Capped at 100% of profit costs and 25% of damages for PI cases).
5. **Our Fees if You Lose:** (Usually zero profit costs; client may still be liable for disbursements).
6. **Disbursements:** How these are funded (Client, After-the-Event (ATE) Insurance, or Solicitor).
7. **The Right to Cancel:** Statutory 14-day cooling-off period for "off-premises" contracts.
8. **Ending the Agreement:** Notice periods and consequences of the client terminating early.
9. **Complaints:** Reference to the firm's complaints procedure and the Legal Ombudsman.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Success Fee:** Must be clearly explained and justified.
- **Informed Consent:** Crucial for the client to understand what they will pay out of their damages.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"This agreement is a conditional fee agreement (CFA) within the meaning of section 58 of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary contract used by solo practitioners to take on cases on a "No Win, No Fee" basis.
