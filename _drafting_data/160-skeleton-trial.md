## Template Type
Skeleton Argument — Trial

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction**: Brief overview of the case and the parties.
2. **Issues**: List of the central legal and factual questions for the court.
3. **Factual Background**: Chronological summary of key facts (cross-referenced to bundle).
4. **Legal Framework**: Summary of relevant statutes and case law.
5. **Submissions**: Detailed arguments on each issue.
6. **Conclusion/Relief**: Statement of the desired outcome and costs.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Length**: Maximum 25 pages (unless permission obtained).
- **Formatting**: A4, 12pt font, 1.5 or double spacing, numbered paragraphs.
- **Citations**: OSCOLA format; neutral citations where available.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The purpose of a skeleton argument is to assist the court by setting out as concisely as practicable the arguments upon which a party intends to rely." — PD 52A (applied analogously).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Provides a disciplined framework to ensure all issues are addressed without exceeding page limits that could result in cost sanctions.
