## Currency Warning

## Template Type
Submissions / Skeleton Argument

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction:** Brief summary of the case and the decision being appealed.
2. **The Facts:** Neutral summary of relevant facts.
3. **The Issues:** The core questions for the appeal court.
4. **Submissions on Grounds:** Detailed argument for each numbered ground of appeal.
5. **Authorities:** Citation of key cases/statutes.
6. **Conclusion:** Summary of the desired outcome.
7. **Time Estimate:** For the hearing.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard Format:** A4, 12pt font, 1.5 or double spacing, wide margins.
- **Citations:** Use neutral citations where available.
- **Cross-Referencing:** Reference the appeal bundle (e.g., [B/12/145]).
- **Brevity:** "A skeleton argument should be a skeleton, not a body." (Judicial guidance).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The appellant's core submission is that the lower court failed to distinguish between [X] and [Y], leading to an erroneous application of the [Regulation Name]."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The most influential document in an appeal. Often, the judge will have formed a preliminary view based solely on the skeleton argument before the hearing begins.
