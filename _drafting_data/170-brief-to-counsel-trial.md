## Template Type
Brief to Counsel — Trial

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading**: Court, Case Name, Claim No., and "BRIEF TO COUNSEL".
2. **Introduction**: Summary of the case and what Counsel is instructed to do.
3. **Background**: Chronological history of the dispute.
4. **The Evidence**: Summary of witness and expert evidence.
5. **Enclosures**: List of documents (Pleadings, Orders, Bundle).
6. **Counsel's Task**: Trial advocacy, opening/closing, cross-examination.
7. **Fees**: Agreed fee/brief fee and refreshers.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Clarity**: Be explicit about the "theory of the case."
- **Formatting**: Numbered paragraphs; double-spaced for Counsel's notes.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A brief should be clear and concise, providing Counsel with all necessary information to present the case effectively." — Bar Council Guidance.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Essential for outsourcing trial advocacy to specialized Counsel while maintaining control of the case.
