## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR Part 24)

## Currency Warning
Note that summary judgment cannot be applied for in certain proceedings, such as those for possession of residential premises against a mortgagor.

## Template Type
Motion / Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Application Notice (N244):** Cross-reference.
2. **The Order Sought:** "The Applicant applies for summary judgment against the [Respondent] on the [Claim/Defence] pursuant to CPR Part 24."
3. **The Grounds (CPR 24.3):**
   - (1) The [Respondent] has **no real prospect** of [succeeding on the claim/successfully defending the claim].
   - (2) There is **no other compelling reason** why the case or issue should be disposed of at a trial.
4. **Evidence:** Usually a Witness Statement setting out why the case is so clear-cut that a trial is unnecessary.
5. **Statement of Truth.**

## Drafting Conventions
- **"No Real Prospect":** The burden is on the applicant to show that the prospect of success is "fanciful" rather than "realistic."
- **Timing:** Generally applied for after the Defence has been filed.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The Court may give summary judgment against a claimant or defendant on the whole of a claim or on a particular issue if it considers that the claimant has no real prospect of succeeding on the claim or issue or the defendant has no real prospect of successfully defending the claim or issue."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A powerful tool to end meritless litigation early and save costs for clients.
