## Currency Warning
High threshold for "real prospect." Ensure no "mini-trial" is attempted in the skeleton.

## Template Type
Skeleton Argument — Summary Judgment

## Structural Skeleton
1. **The Threshold**: "No real prospect of succeeding on the claim or issue."
2. **No Other Compelling Reason**: Why the case should not go to trial.
3. **Factual Indicia**: Key undisputed facts that determine the outcome.
4. **Legal Principles**: Relevant case law (e.g., *Swain v Hillman*).
5. **Response to Defence**: Addressing why the defence is "fanciful."

## Drafting Conventions
- **Focus**: Demonstrate the lack of a triable issue.
- **Evidence**: Point to contemporaneous documents that contradict the other side.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The court may give summary judgment against a claimant or defendant on the whole of a claim or on a particular issue if... it considers that [the party] has no real prospect of succeeding." — CPR 24.2.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard tool to dispose of meritless claims/defences early, saving client costs.
