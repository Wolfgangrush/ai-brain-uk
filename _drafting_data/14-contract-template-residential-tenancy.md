## Authority Tier
Government Model Agreement / Statutory Framework (Housing Act 1988)

## Currency Warning
Divergence: Use Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST) for England. Use "Standard Occupation Contract" for Wales (Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016). Note: The Renters' Rights Bill 2024 is expected to significantly alter ASTs in England.

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Landlord and Tenant(s).
2. **The Property:** Address and included furniture/parking.
3. **The Term:** Fixed term (e.g., 6 or 12 months) and start date.
4. **The Rent:** Amount, frequency, and payment method.
5. **The Deposit:** Amount (capped at 5 weeks' rent) and the Protection Scheme details (DPS/TDS/MyDeposits).
6. **Tenant's Obligations:** Payment of rent, utilities, council tax; maintenance of interior; no illegal acts; no subletting without consent.
7. **Landlord's Obligations:** Quiet enjoyment; repair of structure and exterior (s.11 LTA 1985); safety certificates (Gas, EPC, EICR).
8. **Ending the Tenancy:** Notice periods (s.21 / s.8 notices).
9. **General:** Governing law, service of notices (s.48 LTA 1987).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Deposit Protection:** Mandatory prescribed information must be served within 30 days of receipt.
- **S.11 LTA 1985:** Implied repair obligations cannot be contracted out of for short leases.
- **Wales:** Must use the "Written Statement" of the Occupation Contract; failing to do so carries penalties.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The Landlord shall keep in repair the structure and exterior of the Property (including drains, gutters and external pipes) and keep in repair and proper working order the installations in the Property for the supply of water, gas and electricity and for sanitation."
