## Authority Tier
Statutory Instrument (Taking Control of Goods Regs 2013)

## Currency Warning
The enforcement officer must provide 7 clear days' notice (Notice of Enforcement) before visiting.

## Template Type
High Court Writ

## Structural Skeleton
1. **The Command:** "ELIZABETH THE SECOND [Update to CHARLES THE THIRD as appropriate]... to the High Court Enforcement Officers... WE COMMAND YOU that of the goods, chattels and other property of [Debtor]... you cause to be made the sum of £[Amount]."
2. **Interest:** Calculation of post-judgment interest at 8%.
3. **Execution Costs:** Added to the debt.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Writ Duration:** Valid for 12 months, can be extended.
- **Exempt Goods:** Tools of trade (up to £1,350) and basic household needs cannot be taken.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"You are commanded to seize in execution the goods, chattels and other property of the judgment debtor to satisfy the judgment debt."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The actual legal "power" behind the HCEO. Understanding the Writ of Control is essential for explaining to clients why the "bailiffs" can or cannot take certain items.
