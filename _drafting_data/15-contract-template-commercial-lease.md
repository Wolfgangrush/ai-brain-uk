## Authority Tier
Statutory Framework (Landlord and Tenant Act 1954)

## Currency Warning
The "security of tenure" provisions under the 1954 Act are complex; ensure the lease explicitly states if it is "contracted out" of the Act (which requires a specific notice and declaration procedure).

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Landlord and Tenant (and Guarantor, if any).
2. **The Property:** Defined premises, including a plan.
3. **The Term:** Fixed term; exclusion or inclusion of 1954 Act protection.
4. **The Rent:** Amount, payment dates (usually "quarter days"), and rent review mechanism (e.g., upwards-only to market value).
5. **Services & Service Charge:** Landlord's obligations to maintain common parts; Tenant's contribution.
6. **Insurance:** Landlord insures; Tenant pays the premium.
7. **Repair & Maintenance:** "Full Repairing and Insuring" (FRI) or limited by a "Schedule of Condition."
8. **Alterations & Signage:** Restrictions on structural/non-structural changes.
9. **Use:** Permitted use class (e.g., E(g)(i) for offices).
10. **Alienation:** Restrictions on assignment, subletting, or sharing possession.
11. **Termination:** Forfeiture for non-payment of rent or breach of covenant.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Quarter Days:** Traditionally 25 March, 24 June, 29 September, and 25 December.
- **Yielding Up:** Clear provisions on the state the property must be in at the end of the term (to avoid "dilapidations" disputes).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The Tenant shall keep the Property in good and substantial repair and condition and shall yield up the Property in such repair and condition at the end of the Term."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Commonly used for shop, office, or industrial unit leases for small business clients.
