## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (CPR Part 31)

## Currency Warning
Used for "Standard Disclosure" in the County Court and non-BPC High Court cases.

## Template Type
Disclosure List

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Section 1:** Documents in the party's control that they do not object to being inspected.
2. **Section 2:** Documents in the party's control that they object to being inspected (e.g., Privileged).
3. **Section 3:** Documents that were, but no longer are, in the party's control.
4. **Disclosure Statement:** Signed statement certifying the search and understanding of the duty.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Brief Description:** Items in Section 1 should be described clearly (e.g., "Email from X to Y dated [Date]").
- **Duty of Search:** Must describe the extent of the search (what was searched, by whom, and using what parameters).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"I certify that I have carried out a reasonable and proportionate search for documents... I understand that the duty of disclosure is a continuing one."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The standard way to provide disclosure in "ordinary" civil litigation. It is a formal document that can lead to sanctions if documents are hidden or "lost."
