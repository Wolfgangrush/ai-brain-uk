## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR Part 16 & Practice Direction 16)

## Currency Warning
Ensure compliance with the latest PD 16 requirements for specific claim types (e.g., personal injury, land, or professional negligence).

## Template Type
Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading:** Court Name, Claim Number, Parties.
2. **Introduction:** "1. This is a claim for [Type of Claim]."
3. **The Parties:** Capacity and relationship (e.g., "At all material times, the Claimant was a consultant...").
4. **The Contract (if applicable):** Date, parties, written/oral/conduct, core terms.
5. **The Breach:** Specific acts or omissions constituting the breach of contract or duty.
6. **Causation:** How the breach led to the loss.
7. **Loss & Damage:** Particulars of loss (often cross-referenced to a Schedule of Loss).
8. **Interest:** Statutory or contractual basis, rate, and calculation.
9. **Relief Sought:** "AND THE CLAIMANT CLAIMS: (1) Damages; (2) Interest; (3) Costs."
10. **Statement of Truth:** Mandatory wording.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Concise Statement of Facts:** Plead facts, not evidence.
- **Numbered Paragraphs:** Mandatory.
- **Appendices:** Attach copies of written contracts (PD 16 para 7.3).
- **Representative Capacity:** Must be stated in the heading.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"By a contract in writing dated [Date] ('the Contract'), the Defendant agreed to [Obligation]. A copy of the Contract is attached to these Particulars of Claim at Annex 1."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to draft the substantive legal and factual basis of a claim, either as part of the N1 form or as a separate document.
