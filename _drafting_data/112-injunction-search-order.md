## Authority Tier
Practice Direction (PD 25A para 7)

## Currency Warning
Search orders (formerly Anton Piller) are the most intrusive orders. A "Supervising Solicitor" (independent of the applicant) MUST be present.

## Template Type
Interim Relief (Search/Seizure)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Access:** Order to allow specified persons to enter premises.
2. **Search:** Power to search for and remove evidence/property.
3. **Supervising Solicitor:** Name and details of the independent solicitor.
4. **Hours of Search:** Usually 9am to 5:30pm.
5. **Exceptions:** Privilege (materials that cannot be seized).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Extremely High Threshold:** Must show "very strong prima facie case" and that "serious damage" will be done if not granted.
- **Specific Premises:** Must be clearly identified.
- **Inventory:** A list of items seized must be provided immediately.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"You must allow the following persons to enter the premises... for the purpose of searching for and removing into the custody of the Applicant's Solicitors the following items..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Extremely rare for solo practitioners unless specializing in IP or fraud. Costs of the supervising solicitor and search team are substantial.
