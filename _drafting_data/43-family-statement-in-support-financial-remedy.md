## Authority Tier
Tier 2: Witness Evidence (FPR PD 9A / PD 22A)

## Currency Warning
Adhere to the 15-page limit prescribed by the Statement on the Efficient Conduct of Financial Remedy Hearings (2022/2026 updates).

## Template Type
Section 25 Witness Statement (Financial Remedy)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** Family Court at [Location], Case No., Parties' names.
2. **Introduction:** Identity of the deponent and purpose of the statement.
3. **Background:** Chronology of the relationship, marriage, and separation.
4. **The Section 25 Factors (Matrimonial Causes Act 1973):**
   - (a) Assets, Income, and Earning Capacity.
   - (b) Financial Needs and Obligations.
   - (c) Standard of Living.
   - (d) Age and Duration of Marriage.
   - (e) Physical or Mental Disabilities.
   - (f) Contributions to the Family.
   - (g) Conduct (rarely relevant).
   - (h) Value of Lost Benefits (e.g., Pensions).
5. **Proposals:** Clear statement of the desired financial settlement.
6. **Statement of Truth:** Per FPR PD 17A.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Evidence-Based:** Every assertion of need should be cross-referenced to the Form E or supporting invoices/bank statements.
- **Neutral Tone:** Avoid emotive or aggressive language; focus on the statutory factors.
- **Formatting:** Use 12pt font, 1.5/double spacing, and numbered paragraphs per PD 27A.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"I, [Name], of [Address], SAY AS FOLLOWS: 1. I make this statement in support of my application for financial remedies dated [Date]... 12. My housing needs are [Details]..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "backbone" of the financial remedy case. For a small firm, ensuring this statement is strictly 15 pages and focused on Section 25 factors prevents judicial irritation and potential cost penalties for non-compliance with the "Efficient Conduct" rules.
