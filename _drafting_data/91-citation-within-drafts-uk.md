Style Guide

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Statutes:** `Short Title Year` (e.g., Human Rights Act 1998). No comma before the year.
2. **Sections:** `s 1` (singular), `ss 1-5` (plural). No full stop after 's'.
3. **Cases:** `*Name* [Year] Court Number`.
4. **Neutral Citation:** Use for all cases from 2001 onwards.
5. **Footnotes:** Marker after the punctuation. Minimal punctuation in the footnote itself.
6. **Paragraph Pinpointing:** Use square brackets `[42]`.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Consistency:** The key to professional drafts is consistent citation.
- **High Court / Court of Appeal:** Distinguish between `[2024] EWHC 123 (KB)` and `[2024] EWCA Civ 123`.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Cite a section of an Act using 's' for section and 'ss' for sections. Leave a space between the 's' and the number."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to ensure that all legal arguments, particulars of claim, and witness statements cite authority in a way that is familiar and professional to judges.
