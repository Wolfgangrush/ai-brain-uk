Practice Direction (PD 57AD)

## Currency Warning
Only applies in the Business and Property Courts (BPC).

## Template Type
Substantive Definitions

## Structural Skeleton
- **Model A:** Disclosure of known adverse documents only.
- **Model B:** Limited Disclosure (Key docs + adverse docs).
- **Model C:** Request-led Disclosure (Searching for specific items/categories).
- **Model D:** Narrow search-based Disclosure (Keywords and date ranges).
- **Model E:** Wide search-based Disclosure (Exceptional; "Train of inquiry").

## Drafting Conventions
- **Model C Requests:** Must be specific and tied to a "List of Issues for Disclosure."
- **Model D Searches:** Must be proportionate.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Model D: The party must disclose documents which are likely to support or adversely affect its own case or that of another party on the Issues for Disclosure."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
In the BPC, you must choose one of these models for every issue. Solo practitioners should lean towards Models B or C to control costs and time.
