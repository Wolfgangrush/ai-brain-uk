## Authority Tier
Practice Direction (PD 25A)

## Currency Warning
Freezing orders (formerly Mareva) are "draconian." The court requires a "good arguable case" and a "real risk of dissipation."

## Template Type
Interim Relief (Asset Freezing)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **The Freeze:** Amount restrained.
2. **Exceptions:**
   - Ordinary living expenses (e.g., £500/week).
   - Reasonable legal costs.
   - Ordinary course of business.
3. **Disclosure:** Respondent must disclose assets above a certain value within 48-72 hours.
4. **Third Parties:** Banks/Institutions must be notified.
5. **Return Date:** Date for the inter-partes hearing.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard Form:** Use the Model Order (NMO3/NMO4 as of 2025).
- **Maximum Sum:** Usually a specific cap is placed on the freeze.
- **Affidavit Evidence:** Mandatory (unlike standard applications which use witness statements).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Respondent must not remove from England and Wales or in any way dispose of, deal with or diminish the value of any of their assets in England and Wales up to the value of £[Amount]."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "nuclear option" to prevent a defendant from becoming "judgment-proof" by hiding money. Requires significant preparation and often a barrister's input.
