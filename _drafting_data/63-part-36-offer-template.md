## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR Part 36)

## Currency Warning
The consequences of a Part 36 offer are automatic and strictly governed. Any deviation from the wording in CPR 36.5 may result in the offer losing its Part 36 status.

## Template Type
Settlement / Offer

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** Court Name, Claim Number, Parties.
2. **The Statement:** "This is a Part 36 offer."
3. **Compliance:** "This offer is made pursuant to and is intended to have the consequences of CPR Part 36."
4. **Scope:** "It relates to [the whole of the claim / a part of the claim namely [Part]]."
5. **Counterclaims:** "It takes into account [the whole / part] of the [Defendant's counterclaim]."
6. **The Relevant Period:** "The Relevant Period is 21 days from the date of service of this offer."
7. **The Sum:** "The [Defendant] offers to pay the [Claimant] the sum of £[Amount] in full and final settlement."
8. **Costs:** Acknowledgement of liability for costs if accepted within the Relevant Period (CPR 36.13).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Writing:** Must be in writing.
- **N242A Form:** While not mandatory, using the official form helps ensure compliance.
- **Indemnity Basis:** If a claimant's Part 36 offer is beaten at trial, they may be entitled to interest on damages and costs on the indemnity basis.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"This offer is intended to have the consequences of Section I of Part 36 of the Civil Procedure Rules."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The most effective tool to exert pressure on the opposing party and protect the client's position regarding costs.
