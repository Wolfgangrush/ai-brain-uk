## Authority Tier
Regulator Template (ACAS) / Statutory Requirement (Employment Rights Act 1996 s.1)

## Currency Warning
Since April 2020, the right to a written statement applies from Day 1 of employment/work for both employees and "workers."

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
**Part 1: The Principal Statement (Day 1)**
1. **Names:** Employer and Employee/Worker.
2. **Start Date:** And date of continuous employment.
3. **Job Title/Description.**
4. **Pay:** Rate and frequency.
5. **Hours of Work:** Including variable hours/days.
6. **Holiday Entitlement.**
7. **Place of Work:** Address or requirement to work at various locations.
8. **Probationary Period:** Duration and conditions.
9. **Benefits:** Insurance, car, etc.
10. **Training:** Mandatory training (paid/unpaid).

**Part 2: Other Particulars (Within 2 Months)**
11. **Notice Period:** For both parties.
12. **Sick Pay:** Terms and conditions.
13. **Other Paid Leave:** Maternity, bereavement, etc.
14. **Pensions:** Auto-enrolment details.
15. **Disciplinary & Grievance Procedures:** Rules and contact persons.
16. **Collective Agreements.**

## Drafting Conventions
- **s.1 ERA 1996:** The "Written Statement of Employment Particulars" is a statutory requirement, not just a contractual choice.
- **Worker vs Employee:** Ensure the template distinguishes between these categories if the client uses contractors/gig workers.
- **Statement of Truth:** Not required for the contract itself, but required for any ET1/ET3 tribunal forms.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Your employment with [Employer Name] began on [Date]. Your period of continuous employment began on [Date]. No period of employment with any previous employer counts as part of your period of continuous employment."

Used to ensure small business clients are compliant with UK employment law for every new hire.
