## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (CPR Part 3 / Practice Direction 3E)

## Currency Warning
Since Oct 2020, "incurred costs" are those up to the date of the first budget. Note the exemption for claims under £50,000 where only the first page is mandatory.

## Template Type
Costs / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Front Page:** Summary of totals for each phase (Pre-action, Issue, Disclosure, Witness Statements, Experts, CCMC, Trial, etc.).
2. **Phase Breakdown (Subsequent Pages):**
   - **Incurred Costs:** (Spent to date).
   - **Estimated Costs:** (Future spending).
   - Break down by: Solicitors' grade (A, B, C, D), hours, rate, and disbursements (counsel's fees, expert fees).
3. **Contingencies:** Specific potential costs (e.g., "Contingent A: Interim Application to Set Aside").
4. **Statement of Truth:** Signed by a senior legal representative.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Proportionality:** Budgets must be proportionate to the value and complexity of the claim.
- **Indemnity Principle:** Estimated costs must not exceed what the client has actually agreed to pay the solicitor.
- **Guideline Hourly Rates (GHR):** Check the latest GHR for the relevant court region (London vs National).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"This budget is a fair and accurate statement of incurred and estimated costs which it would be reasonable and proportionate for my client to incur in this litigation."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Mandatory in almost all multi-track civil litigation; failure to file correctly can result in costs being limited to court fees only.
