## Structural Skeleton
1. **Chapter 1: General Matters** - Bound by Rules, use of official forms.
2. **Chapter 2: Open Justice** - Public access and note-taking.
3. **Chapter 4: Custody and Bail** - Forfeiture and surrender.
4. **Chapter 5: Trial & Listing** - Use of Live Links (Section 51 CJA 2003).
5. **Chapter 6: Vulnerable People** - Special Measures (Ground Rules Hearings).
6. **Chapter 7: Expert Evidence** - Admissibility and the Duty to the Court.
7. **Chapter 9: Sentencing**
   - **9.1-9.4:** Basis of Plea requirements (Written/Signed).
   - **9.5:** Victim Personal Statements (VPS).
8. **Chapter 10: Appeals** - Skeleton arguments and citation in the CACD.

## Drafting Conventions
- **CrimPR Alignment:** The Directions supplement the Rules; where a Rule says "the court may," the Direction usually explains "how."
- **Forms:** Chapter 1 mandates the use of digital forms via the Common Platform / DCS.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Essential for trial preparation, particularly Chapter 6 (Vulnerable Witnesses) and Chapter 7 (Experts). For a small firm, following the Chapter 9 Basis of Plea rules is vital for effective sentencing advocacy.
