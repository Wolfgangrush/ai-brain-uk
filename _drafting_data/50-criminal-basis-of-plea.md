## Authority Tier
Tier 2: Procedural Document (Criminal Practice Directions 2023, Chapter 9)

## Currency Warning
Must comply with CPD 2023 requirements: must be in writing, signed by the defendant and advocate, and served on the Prosecution and Court.

## Template Type
Basis of Plea (Guilty Plea Factual Matrix)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Case Header:** Court, Case Number, Defendant Name, and Counts on Indictment.
2. **Admission:** Formal entry of guilty plea to specific counts.
3. **Factual Admission:** Precise description of the admitted actus reus and mens rea.
4. **Disputed Facts:** Explicit list of prosecution allegations that are NOT admitted.
5. **Sentencing Impact:** Submission on the relevant sentencing guidelines category.
6. **Declaration:** Acknowledgement of the court's power to hold a Newton Hearing.
7. **Signatures:** Defendant and Defence Advocate.
8. **Prosecution Position:** Acceptance, non-acceptance, or "immaterial" status.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Surgical Precision:** Do not include irrelevant "mitigation" here; keep it to the facts of the offence.
- **Consistency:** Ensure the basis does not contradict the core of the offence (which would make the plea "equivocal").
- **Clarity:** Use numbered paragraphs to allow the Prosecution to easily identify which parts they accept.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Defendant enters a plea of GUILTY to Count 1 on the following basis: 1. The Defendant admits hitting the victim once with an open palm. 2. The Defendant denies the use of a weapon or any kicking while the victim was on the ground."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Crucial for narrowing the issues at sentencing. If the Prosecution does not accept the basis, the solo advocate must be prepared for a "Newton Hearing" (a mini-trial on the facts).
