## Template Type
Judicial Review — Summary Grounds of Resistance

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction**: Defendant's position (e.g., "The claim is unarguable").
2. **Factual Corrections**: Addressing inaccuracies in the Claimant's grounds.
3. **Response to Grounds**: Point-by-point rebuttal of illegality, irrationality, etc.
4. **Alternative Remedy**: Argue that the Claimant has not exhausted other routes.
5. **Academic/Mootness**: If the decision has already been superseded.
6. **Conclusion**: Why permission should be refused.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Conciseness**: Keep it brief (typically 5-10 pages).
- **Duty of Candour**: Remind the client of the duty to disclose all relevant facts.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The summary grounds must set out as concisely as possible the basis on which the defendant intends to resist the claim." — PD 54A.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Efficiently protects a public body client from the costs of a full JR hearing.
