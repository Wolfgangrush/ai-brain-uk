## Currency Warning
Post-2019 guidance from the SRA and ACAS emphasizes that NDAs cannot be used to prevent "protected disclosures" (whistleblowing) or reporting crimes to the police.

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Disclosing Party, Receiving Party (and "Permitted Recipients").
2. **Definition of Confidential Information:** Include documents, data, oral disclosures, and the fact that discussions are taking place.
3. **The Permitted Purpose:** Specific reason for disclosure (e.g., "evaluating a potential business acquisition").
4. **Non-Disclosure Obligations:** Core covenant not to disclose or use for other purposes.
5. **Permitted Disclosures:** Carve-outs for professional advisors, employees on a "need to know" basis, and legal/regulatory requirements.
6. **Whistleblowing Carve-out:** Mandatory clause ensuring nothing prevents a protected disclosure under PIDA 1998.
7. **Return or Destruction of Information:** Upon request or termination.
8. **Term & Survival:** Duration of confidentiality (often 2-5 years post-termination).
9. **No Intellectual Property Rights:** Clarify that disclosure does not grant a license or title.
10. **Remedies:** Acknowledgement that damages may be inadequate and injunctive relief may be sought.
11. **Boilerplate:** Governing law (England & Wales), Third Party Rights.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Specificity:** Avoid "all information" without some context; courts may find overly broad NDAs unenforceable.
- **Duration:** Perpetual confidentiality is difficult to enforce in commercial contexts unless it relates to trade secrets.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Nothing in this agreement shall preclude the Receiving Party from making a protected disclosure within the meaning of Part IVA of the Employment Rights Act 1996 or from reporting a suspected crime to any relevant law enforcement agency."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Commonly drafted for startups, inventors, or businesses entering preliminary negotiations for a sale or partnership.
