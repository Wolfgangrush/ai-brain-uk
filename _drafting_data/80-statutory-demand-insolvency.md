## Authority Tier
Statutory Form (Insolvency (England and Wales) Rules 2016 - Form 4.1 / SD1)

## Currency Warning
For individual debtors, use Form 6.1. For companies, use Form 4.1 (SD1). Statutory demands are a precursor to winding-up/bankruptcy petitions and should not be used if the debt is "genuinely disputed."

## Template Type
Statutory Notice

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Debtor Information:** Name and registered office address of the company.
2. **The Debt:**
   - Amount of debt.
   - Consideration (how the debt arose, e.g., unpaid invoices).
   - Any interest or additional charges.
3. **The Demand:** Formal demand for payment within 21 days.
4. **Consequences:** Warning that failure to pay may lead to a winding-up petition.
5. **Creditor Contact Details:** Where to pay or who to contact.
6. **Authentication:** Signed by the creditor or their solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Liquidated Sum:** The debt must be for a certain, non-contingent amount (minimum £750 for companies).
- **Service:** Should be served on the registered office.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The creditor demands that the company do, within 21 days of the date of service of this demand, pay the debt or secure or compound for it to the reasonable satisfaction of the creditor."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to pressure companies into paying undisputed debts or as a formal step before insolvency proceedings.
