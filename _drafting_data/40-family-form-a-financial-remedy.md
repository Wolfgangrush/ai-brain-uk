## Template Type
Notice of Intention to Proceed with Financial Remedy Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Case Details:** Court name, case number, and full names of Applicant and Respondent.
2. **Prayer for Relief:** Selection of specific orders sought (e.g., Periodical Payments, Lump Sum, Property Adjustment, Pension Sharing).
3. **Children Details:** Names and dates of birth of any children of the family.
4. **MIAM Certificate:** Confirmation of attendance at a Mediation Information and Assessment Meeting or valid exemption code.
5. **Service Details:** Address for service for both parties.
6. **Statement of Truth:** Mandatory verification by the Applicant or their legal representative.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Precision:** Tick only the boxes for orders that are realistically sought; over-claiming can lead to costs scrutiny.
- **MIAM Compliance:** The MIAM section is a "gatekeeper" requirement; the court will likely strike out applications without a valid certificate or exemption.
- **Electronic Submission:** In the MyHMCTS portal, the "Form A" is generated via a series of digital questions rather than a static PDF upload.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"I, the [Applicant][Respondent] intend to apply for [a financial order][an order for maintenance pending outcome of proceedings]... The grounds for my application are [set out briefly]..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to formally trigger the "contested" financial remedy process after a divorce or dissolution application has reached the conditional order stage. For solo practitioners, the MyHMCTS portal streamlines the filing but requires strict adherence to the digital workflow.
