## Authority Tier
Practice Direction (PD 35 para 2)

## Currency Warning
Instructions are NOT privileged. The court can order their disclosure if they are inaccurate or incomplete (CPR 35.10(4)).

## Template Type
Professional Correspondence

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Status:** Confirming the expert's duty is to the court, not the instructing party.
2. **The Issues:** List the specific questions the expert is asked to address.
3. **The Facts:** Provide a neutral background and list of documents provided.
4. **The Materials:** List all evidence/data the expert should rely on.
5. **Format:** Request the report be compliant with PD 35.
6. **Timeline:** Deadline for the draft and final report.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Neutrality:** Avoid leading questions or "coaching" the expert.
- **Clarity:** Ensure the expert knows exactly what they are (and are not) being asked to do.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Your primary duty is to the court. This duty overrides any obligation to the person from whom you have received instructions or by whom you are paid."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The foundation of expert evidence. A poor letter of instruction leads to a poor report, which can be excluded by the court.
