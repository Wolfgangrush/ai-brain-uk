## Authority Tier
Practice Direction (CPR PD 52A para 4)

## Currency Warning
Grounds must be concise and state exactly why the decision was "wrong" or "unjust" (CPR 52.21(3)).

## Template Type
Appellate Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading:** Claim Number and Appeal Reference.
2. **Identification:** Clearly state which parts of the order are being appealed.
3. **Categorization:** Identify whether the error is one of Law, Fact, or Discretion.
4. **Numbered Grounds:**
   - Ground 1: Error of Law (e.g., misapplication of a specific statute).
   - Ground 2: Error of Fact (e.g., finding of fact not supported by evidence).
   - Ground 3: Procedural Irregularity (unjust decision).
5. **Relief Sought:** Specify if the appellant wants the order set aside, varied, or a new trial.

## Drafting Conventions
- **No Argument:** Do not plead the "why" in terms of narrative; keep it strictly to the "what" (the error).
- **Paragraph Numbers:** Must correspond to the skeleton argument for easy cross-referencing.
- **Tanfern Principles:** Remember that the threshold is "real prospect of success."

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The learned Judge erred in law by failing to apply the principle in [Case Name] regarding [Legal Issue], and consequently arrived at a decision that was plainly wrong."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for framing the scope of the appeal. The court will rarely allow an appellant to raise points at the hearing that were not explicitly in the Grounds.
