## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR 35.12)

## Currency Warning
The meeting is "without prejudice." The joint statement itself is disclosable and binding on the experts.

## Template Type
Expert Coordination

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Intro:** Names of experts and their fields.
2. **Table of Agreement:**
   - Point 1: Agreed.
   - Point 2: Agreed.
3. **Table of Disagreement:**
   - Point 3: Expert A says [X], Expert B says [Y]. Reasons for disagreement.
4. **Signatures:** Both experts must sign.

## Drafting Conventions
- **No Lawyers:** Lawyers should NOT attend the meeting or draft the joint statement (unless specifically permitted by the court).
- **Narrowing:** The goal is to reduce the number of issues the judge has to decide at trial.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The experts have met and discussed the issues... The following is a summary of the points upon which they have reached agreement and those upon which they remain in disagreement."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A vital tool for saving trial time. Small firms should ensure their expert is well-prepared for the meeting and understands they are not there to "win" but to assist the court.
