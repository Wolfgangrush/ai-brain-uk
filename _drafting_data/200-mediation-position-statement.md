## Template Type
ADR — Mediation Position Statement

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading**: Parties, Mediator, Date, and WP warning.
2. **Introduction**: Brief overview of the dispute and representatives attending.
3. **Factual Background**: Chronological summary from the client's perspective.
4. **Key Issues**: Liability, Quantum, and Procedural status.
5. **Settlement History**: Brief mention of previous offers.
6. **Interests**: Identifying non-legal objectives (e.g., commercial relationship).
7. **Conclusion**: Commitment to good faith negotiation.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Tone**: Constructive and commercial, not aggressive.
- **Length**: 5-10 pages; use appendices for complex chronologies.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The position statement is for the mediator and the other party, designed to facilitate a settlement rather than win a legal argument."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary document for mediation. Allows the solicitor to communicate directly with the opposing party's decision-maker.
