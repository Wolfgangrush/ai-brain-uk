## Currency Warning
Redactions should be kept to the absolute minimum. Over-redaction is a common source of court complaints.

## Template Type
Drafting Protocol

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Grounds for Redaction:**
   - Irrelevant and Sensitive (e.g., personal bank details).
   - Privilege (redacting privileged parts of an otherwise disclosable doc).
   - Commercial Secrecy (rarely allowed without a confidentiality ring).
2. **Method:** Black-out or white-out? Must be permanent.
3. **Notation:** Every redaction should be labeled with the reason (e.g., [Redacted - Privilege]).
4. **Certificate of Redaction:** A letter or statement confirming the reasons for redaction.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Integrity:** The context of the document must still be understandable where possible.
- **Unredacted Version:** Must be prepared for the judge if the redactions are challenged.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Claimant has redacted certain parts of the emails produced in Section 1 on the grounds that they contain commercially sensitive information unrelated to the issues in this claim."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Necessary when a client's disclosable documents contain embarrassing or irrelevant personal data (like medical history or salary details of non-parties).
