## Structural Skeleton
1. **Core Procedure & Case Management**
   - **PD 3A:** MIAMs (Mediation Information and Assessment Meetings) - *Critical Gatekeeper*.
   - **PD 4A:** Striking Out - Rules for dismissing weak applications.
   - **PD 5A:** Forms - The master list of HMCTS family forms.
2. **Service & Jurisdiction**
   - **PD 6A/6B:** Service within/outside UK.
   - **PD 6D:** Service in Refuges (2024 Update) - Protections for domestic abuse survivors.
3. **Financial Proceedings**
   - **PD 9A:** Financial Remedy Application workflow.
   - **PD 9B:** Communication of information.
4. **Children Proceedings**
   - **PD 12B:** The Child Arrangements Programme (CAP) - *The primary Private Law roadmap*.
   - **PD 12J:** Domestic Abuse and Harm - *Mandatory risk assessment framework*.
5. **Evidence & Experts**
   - **PD 22A:** Written Evidence / Witness Statements.
   - **PD 25A-D:** Use of Experts (Psychologists, Accountants, etc.).
6. **Pilot Schemes (Part 36)**
   - **PD 36R:** Private Law Digital Pilot.
   - **PD 36G:** Financial Remedy Digital Pilot.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Cross-Referencing:** Always link specific drafts (e.g., C100) to their governing PD (e.g., PD 12B).
- **MIAM Compliance:** PD 3A is the most frequent point of failure for new applications.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Acts as a high-level "cheat sheet" to identify which specific rule-set governs a client's current procedural stage. Prevents the error of using CPR rules in Family Court.
