## Authority Tier
Practice Direction (PD 57AD - formerly PD 51U)

## Currency Warning
The Disclosure Pilot is now permanent for the Business and Property Courts (BPC) as PD 57AD. "Standard Disclosure" does not apply in the BPC.

## Template Type
Procedural Form (DRD)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Section 1A:** List of Issues for Disclosure.
2. **Section 1B:** Disclosure Models proposed (A to E).
3. **Section 2:** Technical details (ESI, search parameters, date ranges).
4. **Section 3:** Costs estimate for disclosure.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Cooperation:** Parties MUST cooperate to complete the DRD.
- **Proportionality:** Disclosure must be limited to what is necessary for the fair resolution of the issues.
- **Models:** Model C (Request-led) is often favored for efficiency.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The parties are required to discuss and, if possible, agree the List of Issues for Disclosure and the Models to be applied to each Issue."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The DRD is the blueprint for disclosure in high-value commercial cases. It requires significant front-loading of work and technical understanding of how a client's data is stored.
