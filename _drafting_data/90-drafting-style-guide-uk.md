Academic Style Guide (Oxford Standard for the Citation of Legal Authorities)

## Currency Warning
Ensure usage of the 4th Edition (2012). Note that OSCOLA 2006 for International Law is a separate supplemental guide.

## Template Type
Style Guide

## Structural Skeleton
1. **General Principles:** Footnotes only; minimal punctuation (no full stops in abbreviations like 'UKHL' or 'AC').
2. **Cases:** *Name* [Year] Court Number, [Year] Vol Report Abbreviation Page.
   - *Example:* *Corr v IBC Vehicles Ltd* [2008] UKHL 13.
3. **Statutes:** Short Title Year (no comma).
   - *Example:* Human Rights Act 1998.
4. **Pinpointing:**
   - Case paragraphs: [42].
   - Statute sections: s 15(1).
5. **Secondary Sources:**
   - Books: Author, *Title* (Edition, Publisher Year).
   - Articles: Author, 'Title' [Year] Journal Page.
6. **Subsequent Citations:** Use 'ibid' for immediate repeats; use 'Short Form (n [Original Footnote Number])' for others.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Pleadings vs Articles:** While OSCOLA is academic, UK courts expect a similar level of rigor and consistency in citing authorities in Particulars of Claim and Skeleton Arguments.
- **Neutral Citation:** Always include the neutral citation (e.g., [2024] EWCA Civ 123) where available.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Abbreviations should not have full stops. For example, use AC, not A.C., and s, not s."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Ensures that all drafts, especially Skeleton Arguments and Particulars of Claim, look professional and conform to the standards expected by the judiciary.
