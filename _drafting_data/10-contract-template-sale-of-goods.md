## Authority Tier
Statutory Form / Implied Terms (Sale of Goods Act 1979)

## Currency Warning
Note the distinction between Business-to-Business (B2B) governed by SGA 1979 and Business-to-Consumer (B2C) governed by Consumer Rights Act 2015. This template focuses on the B2B framework.

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Full names and registered addresses of Seller and Buyer.
2. **Definitions & Interpretation:** Key terms (Goods, Price, Delivery Date).
3. **Basis of Sale:** Agreement to sell and purchase.
4. **Description of Goods:** Detailed specification (Reference to Schedule).
5. **Price & Payment:** Amount, VAT, payment terms (e.g., 30 days from invoice), interest on late payment.
6. **Delivery:** Location, timing, "time of the essence" status.
7. **Risk & Property (Retention of Title):** Risk passes on delivery; Title passes on full payment (Romalpa clause).
8. **Warranties & Liability:**
   - Compliance with Description (s.13 SGA).
   - Satisfactory Quality (s.14(2) SGA).
   - Fitness for Purpose (s.14(3) SGA).
   - Limitation of Liability (subject to UCTA 1977 reasonableness).
9. **Termination:** Material breach, insolvency.
10. **Force Majeure.**
11. **General:** Governing Law (England & Wales), Jurisdiction, Entire Agreement, Third Party Rights (Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Retention of Title:** Must be explicitly drafted if the seller wants to recover goods upon buyer insolvency.
- **Exclusion of Implied Terms:** Under UCTA 1977 s.6(3), exclusions of s.13-15 SGA are only valid if they pass the "reasonableness test."
- **Section 12 (Title):** Can NEVER be excluded.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The Seller warrants that the Goods will: (a) correspond with their description; (b) be of satisfactory quality within the meaning of the Sale of Goods Act 1979; and (c) be fit for any purpose held out by the Seller or made known to the Seller by the Buyer."
