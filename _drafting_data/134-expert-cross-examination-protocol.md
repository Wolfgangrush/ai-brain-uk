## Currency Warning
Permission to call an expert to give oral evidence is NOT automatic. It must be granted by the court (usually at the CCMC).

## Template Type
Trial Preparation Protocol

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Focus:** Cross-examination should be limited to points of disagreement in the joint statement.
2. **Questions:** Must be put to the expert first in writing (CPR 35.6) if they relate to clarification.
3. **Expert's Duty:** Remains to the court throughout the trial.
4. **Cost Consequences:** If cross-examination is found to be unnecessary or unreasonable, costs may be awarded against the cross-examining party.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Directness:** Questions should be clear and based on the expert's report.
- **No Ambush:** "Trial by ambush" is discouraged; the expert should know the general lines of attack.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Cross-examination of the expert will be restricted to the following issues where the experts have failed to reach agreement."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Solo advocates must be surgical in cross-examination. Attacking an expert's credibility is rarely successful; it is better to attack their assumptions or the data they were provided.
