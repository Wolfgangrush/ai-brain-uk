## Authority Tier
Tier 2 — Contractual / Procedural

## Currency Warning
A Deed requires specific execution formalities (witnessing). Use as an alternative to a Tomlin Order for private settlements.

## Template Type
ADR — Settlement Deed

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties**: Full names and registered addresses.
2. **Recitals**: Background of the dispute and the claim.
3. **Settlement Sum**: Amount, timing, and method of payment.
4. **Release**: Comprehensive release of all claims related to the dispute.
5. **Confidentiality**: Non-disclosure of settlement terms.
6. **No Admissions**: Statement that the settlement is not an admission of liability.
7. **Execution**: Signed as a Deed by all parties.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Precision**: Define "the Dispute" clearly to avoid future litigation.
- **Tax**: Consider the tax implications of the settlement payment.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"In consideration of the payment of the Settlement Sum, the Claimant releases the Defendant from all claims..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Finalizes a dispute without the need for further court involvement. Ideal for out-of-court settlements.
