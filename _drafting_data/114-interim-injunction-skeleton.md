## Currency Warning

## Template Type
Urgent Submissions

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Executive Summary:** Why is the order needed TODAY?
2. **Serious Question:** Brief merits of the underlying claim.
3. **Inadequacy of Damages:** Why money won't fix it later (e.g., loss of "one-off" opportunity).
4. **Balance of Convenience:** Weighing the harm to the Applicant vs the Respondent.
5. **Full and Frank Disclosure Summary:** Highlighting potential defenses and explaining why they don't defeat the application.
6. **The Draft Order:** Walkthrough of the key terms.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Urgency:** Use bold headings for key points.
- **Simplicity:** Don't over-complicate the law; focus on the risk of immediate harm.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The balance of convenience overwhelmingly favors the grant of this injunction, as the Applicant's business will be destroyed by the unauthorized use of the data, whereas the Respondent suffers only a temporary restraint on its trade."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The roadmap for the oral hearing. In "without notice" applications, the judge will often use this as a checklist for their decision.
