## Currency Warning
An undertaking to the court is a solemn promise. Breach is contempt of court.

## Template Type
Mandatory Procedural Undertaking

## Structural Skeleton
1. **The Promise:** "The Applicant undertakes to the court..."
2. **Scope of Damages:** "...to abide by any order the court may make as to damages if the court later finds that this order has caused loss to the Respondent..."
3. **Fortification:** (If required) A bank guarantee or payment into court to "back" the undertaking.
4. **Service Undertaking:** To serve the documents and the order as soon as practicable.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard Clause:** Included in every interim injunction application.
- **Limited Wealth:** If the applicant is a "man of straw," the court may refuse the injunction unless the undertaking is fortified.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"If the court later finds that this order has caused loss to the Respondent, and decides that the Respondent should be compensated for that loss, the Applicant will comply with any order the court may make."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Solo advocates must explain the personal risk to the client. The client is effectively putting their own assets on the line to secure the interim relief.
