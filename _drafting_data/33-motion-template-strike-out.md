## Authority Tier
Court Rule (CPR 3.4)

## Currency Warning
Strike out is a "draconian" remedy; the court will often give a party an opportunity to "amend" their statement of case rather than striking it out immediately.

## Template Type
Motion / Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Application Notice (N244):** Cross-reference.
2. **The Order Sought:** "An order that the [Particulars of Claim / Defence] be struck out pursuant to CPR 3.4(2)."
3. **The Grounds (CPR 3.4(2)):**
   - (a) Discloses **no reasonable grounds** for bringing or defending the claim.
   - (b) Is an **abuse of the court’s process** or is otherwise likely to obstruct the just disposal of the proceedings.
   - (c) Failure to comply with a rule, practice direction, or court order.
4. **Evidence:** Witness statement (especially for grounds (b) and (c)). Ground (a) is usually argued on the face of the pleading itself.
5. **Statement of Truth.**

## Drafting Conventions
- **Legally Incoherent:** Use ground (a) if the pleading, even if every fact were true, would still fail as a matter of law.
- **Vexatious Litigation:** Use ground (b) for repeated or malicious claims.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The court may strike out a statement of case if it appears to the court that the statement of case discloses no reasonable grounds for bringing or defending the claim."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to eliminate legally defective or abusive pleadings at an early stage.
