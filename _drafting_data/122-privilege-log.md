## Template Type
Litigation Table / Log

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Doc ID:** Unique reference number.
2. **Date:** Of the document.
3. **From/To:** Names and roles (to identify if legal counsel was involved).
4. **Type of Privilege:** Legal Advice Privilege (LAP) or Litigation Privilege (LP).
5. **Basis for Claim:** Brief justification (e.g., "Confidential communication for the purpose of seeking legal advice").

## Drafting Conventions
- **Litigation Privilege:** Only applies if litigation is "reasonably in prospect" and the doc was created for the "dominant purpose" of that litigation.
- **Legal Advice Privilege:** Limited to communications between the "client" (as narrowly defined) and the "lawyer."

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The documents listed in Section 2 of Form N265 are withheld from inspection on the grounds that they are subject to Legal Professional Privilege."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used when the opponent challenges your "blanket" claim of privilege in Section 2 of N265. Essential for transparency and avoiding "satellite" litigation over disclosure.
