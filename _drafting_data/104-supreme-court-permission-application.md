## Authority Tier
Supreme Court Practice Direction (PD 3)

## Currency Warning
The test is much higher than for lower appeals: the case must raise an "arguable point of law of general public importance."

## Template Type
Supreme Court Pleading (Form 1)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Appellant and Respondent details.
2. **Order Appealed:** Date and court (usually Court of Appeal).
3. **Certificate of Service:** Mandatory.
4. **Grounds for Permission:**
   - Statement of the point of law.
   - Demonstration of "General Public Importance."
   - Summary of the argument.
5. **Declaration:** Signed by the appellant or their legal representative.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Limit:** Generally 10 pages for the grounds of the application.
- **Public Importance:** This is the make-or-break section. Why does this case matter to people other than the parties?
- **Electronic Filing:** Must be filed via the Supreme Court Portal.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This application raises a point of law of general public importance, namely whether the doctrine of [Name] applies to [Scenario], a matter which affects thousands of [Subject Class] in the UK."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The absolute pinnacle of UK litigation. For a solo practitioner, this usually requires instructing a specialist silk (KC) given the extreme rigor required.
