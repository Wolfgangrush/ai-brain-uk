## Authority Tier
Tier 2: Procedural Notice (CrimPR Rule 14.7)

## Currency Warning
This notice must be served at least 2 business days before the hearing. It is required for any application to vary or grant bail outside a scheduled hearing.

## Template Type
Notice of Application to Consider Bail

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Case Details:** Court, Case No., Defendant, Current Status (Custody/Bail).
2. **Application Nature:** Grant, Vary, or Withdraw Bail.
3. **Previous Decisions:** Summary of previous bail applications and the court's reasons for refusal.
4. **New Information/Argument:** Mandatory section identifying what has changed since the last decision (e.g., new address, surety, or change in case strength).
5. **Proposed Conditions:** Detailed list (Residence, Reporting, Curfew, Non-Contact, Surety).
6. **Service/Hearing:** Confirmation of service on the Court and Prosecution.

## Drafting Conventions
- **The "Change of Circumstances" Rule:** In the Magistrates' Court, you generally only get two "bites" at bail unless you can show a genuine change in circumstances.
- **Mitigation of Risk:** Address the Bail Act 1976 exceptions (Absconding, Further Offences, Witness Interference) directly with the proposed conditions.
- **Surety:** Provide the name and financial status of any surety clearly to allow the Police to vet them.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Defendant applies for bail on the following grounds: 1. There is a material change in circumstances since the last hearing on [Date], namely [Details]... 2. The following conditions are proposed to mitigate the risk of absconding..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to secure a defendant's release between hearings. A solo advocate must ensure the "New Information" section is robust to avoid the application being dismissed without a full hearing.
