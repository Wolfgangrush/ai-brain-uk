## Scope of Digest
- **Rules Covered:** Part 1 (Overriding Objective), Part 6 (Service), Part 31 (Disclosure), Part 36 (Offers to Settle).
- **Rules Deferred:** Specialized parts (Part 45 Fixed Costs, Part 54 Judicial Review, etc.) deferred for high-level "backbone" coverage.
- **Why:** To provide the essential procedural "handbook" for civil litigation.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Rule 1.1 — The overriding objective

**Plain-English summary:** The core rule: courts must deal with cases justly and at proportionate cost.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) These Rules are a procedural code with the overriding objective of enabling the court to deal with cases justly and at proportionate cost.
> (2) Dealing with a case justly and at proportionate cost includes, so far as is practicable –
> (a) ensuring that the parties are on an equal footing...
> (b) saving expense;
> (c) dealing with the case in ways which are proportionate...
> (f) promoting or using alternative dispute resolution.

**Citation format:** CPR 1.1

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Must be cited in almost every procedural application (e.g., for an extension of time or relief from sanctions).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Rule 6.26 — Deemed service

**Plain-English summary:** When a document is legally considered to have arrived, regardless of when it was actually read.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> A document, other than a claim form, served within the United Kingdom... is deemed to be served as follows:
> - First class post...: The second day after it was posted... provided that day is a business day.
> - Fax / Electronic method: If transmitted... before 4.30pm on a business day, on that day.

**Citation format:** CPR 6.26

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Essential for calculating deadlines for defenses or witness statements. Missing a "deemed service" date is a common source of negligence claims.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Rule 31.6 — Standard disclosure

**Plain-English summary:** Requires parties to share documents that help or hurt either side's case.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> Standard disclosure requires a party to disclose only –
> (a) the documents on which he relies; and
> (b) the documents which –
> (i) adversely affect his own case;
> (ii) adversely affect another party’s case; or
> (iii) support another party’s case.

**Citation format:** CPR 31.6

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The default level of disclosure. Note the "Proportionality" filter in CPR 31.7.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Rule 36.5 — Form and content of a Part 36 offer

**Plain-English summary:** Sets the strict requirements for a formal settlement offer that carries cost penalties if rejected.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A Part 36 offer must –
> (a) be in writing;
> (b) make clear that it is made pursuant to Part 36;
> (c) specify a period of not less than 21 days...
> (d) state whether it relates to the whole of the claim or to part of it.

**Citation format:** CPR 36.5

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The most powerful tactical tool in civil litigation. A valid Part 36 offer can force a settlement by creating significant financial risk for the opponent.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
