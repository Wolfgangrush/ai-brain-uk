## Scope of Digest
- **Sections Covered:** 4 (Protected Characteristics), 13 (Direct), 15 (Disability-arising), 19 (Indirect), 26 (Harassment), 27 (Victimisation), 47-48 (Barristers/Advocates).
- **Sections Deferred:** Part 1 (Public Sector Equality Duty) and Part 11 (Advancement of Equality) deferred.
- **Why:** Focuses on the operative definitions of discrimination and specific protections for the legal profession.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 4 — The protected characteristics

**Plain-English summary:** Lists the 9 characteristics protected by law.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> The following characteristics are protected characteristics—
> * age;
> * disability;
> * gender reassignment;
> * marriage and civil partnership;
> * pregnancy and maternity;
> * race;
> * religion or belief;
> * sex;
> * sexual orientation.

**Citation format:** s 4 EqA 2010

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 13 — Direct discrimination

**Plain-English summary:** Treating someone less favourably than others because of a protected characteristic.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person (A) discriminates against another (B) if, because of a protected characteristic, A treats B less favourably than A treats or would treat others.

**Citation format:** s 13 EqA 2010

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Core definition for most discrimination claims. Cannot be "justified" (except for age).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 19 — Indirect discrimination

**Plain-English summary:** Applying a rule or practice that puts people with a protected characteristic at a disadvantage, unless it's a "proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim."

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person (A) discriminates against another (B) if A applies to B a provision, criterion or practice which is discriminatory in relation to a relevant protected characteristic of B's.
> (2) ...a provision, criterion or practice is discriminatory... if—
> ... (d) A cannot show it to be a proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim.

**Citation format:** s 19 EqA 2010

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to challenge neutral-looking policies (e.g., "must work full time") that disproportionately affect certain groups (e.g., women with childcare responsibilities).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 26 — Harassment

**Plain-English summary:** Unwanted conduct related to a characteristic that violates dignity or creates an offensive environment.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person (A) harasses another (B) if—
> (a) A engages in unwanted conduct related to a relevant protected characteristic, and
> (b) the conduct has the purpose or effect of—
> (i) violating B's dignity, or
> (ii) creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating or offensive environment for B.

**Citation format:** s 26 EqA 2010

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Note: *Worker Protection (Amendment of Equality Act 2010) Act 2023* added a proactive duty for employers to prevent sexual harassment.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 47 — Barristers / Section 48 — Advocates

**Plain-English summary:** Specifically prohibits discrimination by barristers and those instructing them.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (6) A person instructing a barrister must not discriminate against the barrister by subjecting the barrister to a detriment.

**Citation format:** s 47 EqA 2010

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Direct protection for solo advocates against discriminatory treatment by solicitors or chambers.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
