## Scope of Digest
- **Sections Covered:** 7, 44, 154, 171-177, 270, 357, 382, 477.
- **Sections Deferred:** Parts 18-47 (Share capital, Debentures, Charges, etc.) deferred as these are specialised corporate finance areas. Focus remains on solo practitioner "Small Company" governance.
- **Why:** To provide the most frequent "actionable" sections for solo advocates managing their own practice or advising small business clients.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 7 — Method of forming company

**Plain-English summary:** Allows a company to be formed by one or more persons. This is the foundation for the "sole shareholder" model used by many solo solicitors.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A company is formed under this Act by one or more persons—
> (a) subscribing their names to a memorandum of association (see section 8), and
> (b) complying with the requirements of this Act as to registration (see sections 9 to 13).

**Citation format:** s 7 CA 2006

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections same statute: s 8 (Memorandum), s 123 (Single member statement)

**Currency:**
- Last amended: N/A (Original enactment)
- Notes: Current consolidated text.

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Forming a personal service company or law firm vehicle.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 44 — Execution of documents

**Plain-English summary:** Specifies how a company validly signs contracts and deeds. For a solo director, it requires the director's signature plus a witness.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (2) A document is validly executed by a company if it is signed on behalf of the company—
> (a) by two authorised signatories, or
> (b) by a director of the company in the presence of a witness who attests the signature.
> (3) The following are “authorised signatories” for the purposes of subsection (2)—
> (a) every director of the company, and
> (b) in the case of a private company with a secretary or a public company, the secretary (or any joint secretary) of the company.

**Citation format:** s 44 CA 2006

**Cross-references:**
- Other statutes: Law of Property (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1989 s 1

**Currency:**
- Last amended: Original text.
- Notes: Note the "Purchaser" protection in s 44(5).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Signing client care letters, leases, or court documents on behalf of the firm.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 154 — Companies required to have directors

**Plain-English summary:** Mandates that a private company must have at least one director (who must be a natural person per s 155).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A private company must have at least one director.
> (2) A public company must have at least two directors.

**Citation format:** s 154 CA 2006

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Minimum governance requirement for a solo practice.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 172 — Duty to promote the success of the company

**Plain-English summary:** The core "fiduciary-lite" duty. A director must act in good faith to promote the success of the company for the members, having regard to stakeholders.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A director of a company must act in the way he considers, in good faith, would be most likely to promote the success of the company for the benefit of its members as a whole, and in doing so have regard (amongst other matters) to—
> (a) the likely consequences of any decision in the long term,
> (b) the interests of the company's employees,
> (c) the need to foster the company's business relationships with suppliers, customers and others,
> (d) the impact of the company's operations on the community and the environment,
> (e) the desirability of the company maintaining a reputation for high standards of business conduct, and
> (f) the need to act fairly as between members of the company.

**Citation format:** s 172 CA 2006

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Re Smith and Fawcett Ltd* [1942] Ch 304 (pre-codification foundation).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The primary lens for all director decisions. Relevant in derivative claims (though rare for solo firms).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 270 — Private company not required to have secretary

**Plain-English summary:** Private companies do not need a company secretary unless their articles say otherwise.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A private company is not required to have a secretary.
> (2) References in the Companies Acts to a private company's secretary are to be read as references to the director or directors of the company, or to any person authorised generally or specifically in that behalf by the director or directors.

**Citation format:** s 270 CA 2006

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Simplifies administrative overhead for solo firms.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 357 — Records of decisions by sole member

**Plain-English summary:** Where a sole member takes a decision that would normally require a general meeting, they must record it in writing.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (2) The member must provide the company with a written record of the decision.
> (3) If the member fails to comply with this section he commits an offence.

**Citation format:** s 357 CA 2006

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Critical compliance for solo operators. Every "shareholder" decision must be minuted/recorded.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 477 — Small companies: conditions for exemption from audit

**Plain-English summary:** Exempts small companies from the requirement to have their accounts audited.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A company that meets the following conditions in respect of a financial year is exempt from the requirements of this Act relating to the audit of accounts for that year.
> (2) The conditions are—
> (a) that the company qualifies as a small company in relation to that year...

**Citation format:** s 477 CA 2006

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 382 (Qualifying as small)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Major cost saving for solo practitioners. Ensure the correct "Audit Exemption" statement is on the balance sheet.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
