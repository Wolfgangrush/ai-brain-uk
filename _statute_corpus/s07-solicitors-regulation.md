## Section-by-Section Digest

### Solicitors Act 1974 — Section 1: Qualifications for practising

**Plain-English summary:** You can only act as a solicitor if you are admitted, on the roll, and have a current practicing certificate.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> No person shall be qualified to act as a solicitor unless—
> (a) he has been admitted as a solicitor, and
> (b) his name is on the roll, and
> (c) he has in force a certificate issued by the Society... authorising him to practise as a solicitor (a “practising certificate”).

**Citation format:** s 1 Solicitors Act 1974

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Foundation of professional standing. Acting without a PC is a criminal offence under s 2.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Legal Services Act 2007 — Section 12: Reserved legal activities

**Plain-English summary:** Lists the activities that only "authorised persons" (like solicitors or barristers) can perform.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) In this Act “reserved legal activity” means—
> (a) the exercise of a right of audience;
> (b) the conduct of litigation;
> (c) reserved instrument activities;
> (d) probate activities;
> (e) notarial activities;
> (f) the administration of oaths.

**Citation format:** s 12 LSA 2007

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Defines the "monopoly" of the legal profession. Essential for distinguishing between "legal advice" (unregulated) and "conduct of litigation" (regulated).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Legal Services Act 2007 — Section 13: Entitlement to carry on a reserved legal activity

**Plain-English summary:** You are only entitled to do the above if you are authorised by a regulator (like the SRA or BSB).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) The question whether a person is entitled to carry on an activity which is a reserved legal activity is to be determined solely in accordance with the provisions of this Act.
> (2) A person is entitled to carry on an activity ("the relevant activity") which is a reserved legal activity if—
> (a) the person is an authorised person in relation to the relevant activity...

**Citation format:** s 13 LSA 2007

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
