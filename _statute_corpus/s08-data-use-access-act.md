## Scope of Digest
- **Sections Covered:** Provisions relating to "Recognised Legitimate Interests", SAR "Stop the Clock", and Automated Decision-Making reforms.
- **Sections Deferred:** Smart Data schemes (Part 1) and NUAR (Part 3) deferred as they are sector-specific (Utilities/Construction).
- **Why:** Focuses on the changes that directly impact general legal practice and data management for solo firms.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 70 — Recognised Legitimate Interests

**Plain-English summary:** Lists specific processing activities (like crime prevention or national security) that are deemed to have a legitimate interest without needing a balancing test.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (2) In Article 6(1) (lawful processing)—
> ...
> (b) after that point insert—
> “(ea) processing is necessary for the purposes of a recognised legitimate interest;”, and
> (c) in the words after point (f), for “Point (f)” substitute “Points (ea) and (f)”.
> ...
> 5. For the purposes of paragraph 1(ea), processing is necessary for the purposes of a recognised legitimate interest only if it meets a condition in Annex 1.

**Citation format:** s 70 DUAA 2025

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Simplifies compliance for firms conducting AML checks or crime prevention activities.

### Section 76 — Subject Access Requests: "Stop the Clock"

**Plain-English summary:** Allows controllers to pause the 30-day response period for a SAR while waiting for the requester to verify their identity or clarify a "vague" request.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (3) After Article 12 insert—
> “Article 12A Meaning of “applicable time period”
> ...
> 5. Where the controller reasonably requires further information in order to identify the information or processing activities to which a request under Article 15 relates—
> (a) the controller may ask the data subject to provide the further information, and
> (b) the period beginning with the day on which the controller makes the request and ending with the day on which the controller receives the information does not count towards—
> (i) the applicable time period, or
> (ii) the period described in paragraph 4(a).”

**Citation format:** s 76 DUAA 2025

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Essential for managing "weaponized" SARs where the scope is excessively broad or the requester's identity is in doubt.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
