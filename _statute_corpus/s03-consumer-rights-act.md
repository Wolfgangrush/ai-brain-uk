English (Authoritative)

## Scope of Digest
- **Sections Covered:** 9-11 (Goods), 19-24 (Remedies for Goods), 49-51 (Services), 62 (Unfair Terms).
- **Sections Deferred:** Part 3 (Enforcement powers) and Schedule 5 (Investigatory powers) deferred as they concern regulators (CMA/Trading Standards).
- **Why:** Focuses on the core statutory rights and remedies most relevant to civil litigation and consumer advice.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 9 — Goods to be of satisfactory quality

**Plain-English summary:** Goods must meet the standard a reasonable person would expect, considering price, description, and durability.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) Every contract to supply goods is to be treated as including a term that the quality of the goods is satisfactory.
> (2) The quality of goods is satisfactory if they meet the standard that a reasonable person would consider satisfactory, taking account of—
> (a) any description of the goods,
> (b) the price or other consideration for the goods (if relevant), and
> (c) all the other relevant circumstances.

**Citation format:** s 9 CRA 2015

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 19 (Remedies)
- Other statutes: Sale of Goods Act 1979 (predecessor for B2B)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The most common basis for consumer "faulty goods" claims.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 19 — Consumer's rights to enforce terms about goods

**Plain-English summary:** Outlines the menu of remedies available when goods are not of satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, or as described.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (3) If the goods do not conform to the contract because of a breach of any of the terms described in sections 9, 10, 11, 13 or 14, the consumer has—
> (a) the short-term right to reject (sections 20 and 22);
> (b) the right to repair or replacement (section 23); and
> (c) the right to a price reduction or the final right to reject (section 24).

**Citation format:** s 19 CRA 2015

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Essential for advising on the "hierarchy" of remedies (e.g., must usually allow one repair/replacement before the final right to reject).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 49 — Service to be performed with reasonable care and skill

**Plain-English summary:** Implies a term into every consumer service contract that the work must be done competently.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) Every contract to supply a service is to be treated as including a term that the trader must perform the service with reasonable care and skill.

**Citation format:** s 49 CRA 2015

**Cross-references:**
- Other statutes: Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982 (B2B predecessor)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Standard for professional negligence or poor workmanship claims in a consumer context.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 62 — Requirement for contract terms and notices to be fair

**Plain-English summary:** Unfair terms in consumer contracts are not legally binding. A term is unfair if it creates a significant imbalance to the consumer's detriment.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) An unfair term of a consumer contract is not binding on the consumer.
> (4) A term is unfair if, contrary to the requirement of good faith, it causes a significant imbalance in the parties' rights and obligations under the contract to the detriment of the consumer.

**Citation format:** s 62 CRA 2015

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to strike out "fine print" clauses that limit liability or impose excessive penalties.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

- **Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008:** Complements CRA with criminal/regulatory sanctions.
