- **Sections Covered:** DPA ss 8, 10, 15, 173; UK GDPR Arts 5, 6, 9, 15.
- **Sections Deferred:** Parts 3 (Law Enforcement) and 4 (Intelligence Services) deferred as they apply to specific state agencies rather than general solo practice.

## Section-by-Section Digest (DPA 2018)

### Section 15 — Exemptions etc

**Plain-English summary:** The "gateway" to exemptions. Schedules 2, 3, and 4 contain the actual restrictions on rights (like SARs).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) Schedules 2, 3 and 4 make provision for exemptions from, and restrictions and adaptations of the application of, rules of the UK GDPR.

**Citation format:** s 15 DPA 2018

**Cross-references:**
- Schedule 2, Part 1, Para 2 (Crime and taxation)
- Schedule 2, Part 2, Para 19 (Legal professional privilege)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to resist disclosure of legally privileged material in response to a Subject Access Request (SAR).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 173 — Altering etc records with intent to prevent disclosure

**Plain-English summary:** Criminal offence to hide or destroy data after a SAR has been made to prevent the person from getting it.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (3) It is an offence for a person to alter, deface, block, erase, destroy or conceal information with the intention of preventing disclosure of all or part of the information that the person making the request would have been entitled to receive.

**Citation format:** s 173 DPA 2018

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Severe warning for clients (and solicitors) when handling SARs in the context of litigation.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Section-by-Section Digest (UK GDPR - Assimilated)

### Article 5 — Principles relating to processing of personal data

**Plain-English summary:** The 7 core principles of data protection: Lawfulness, Purpose limitation, Data minimisation, Accuracy, Storage limitation, Integrity, and Accountability.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 1. Personal data shall be:
> (a) processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner...
> (b) collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes...
> (c) adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary...
> (f) processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security...
> 2. The controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, paragraph 1 (‘accountability’).

**Citation format:** Art 5 UK GDPR

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Article 15 — Right of access by the data subject

**Plain-English summary:** The right to obtain a copy of your personal data from a controller (the "SAR").

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 1. The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed, and, where that is the case, access to the personal data...

**Citation format:** Art 15 UK GDPR

**Currency Note:** Amended by DUAA 2025 to allow "stop the clock" for verification.

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Frequently used by opposing parties as "pre-action discovery". Solicitors must know the LPP exemptions in DPA 2018 Sch 2 to protect their files.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
