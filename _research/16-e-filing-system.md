## Currency Warning
**CE-File** is now mandatory for legally represented parties in the High Court Business and Property Courts (PD 51O). The older **MCOL** system is gradually being integrated into the newer "Online Civil Money Claims" (OCMC) platform.

## Key Facts
1. **CE-File (High Court):** Used for filing new claims and documents in the Business and Property Courts, King's Bench Division, and Court of Appeal.
2. **Mandatory Filing:** Under Practice Direction 51O, legally represented parties **must** use CE-File for most High Court claims.
3. **MCOL (County Court):** A legacy but still active system for simple, specified money claims up to £100,000.
4. **OCMC (Online Civil Money Claims):** The newer, more user-friendly County Court system for money claims, designed to replace MCOL.
5. **Document Specs:** CE-File typically has a **50MB** limit per document. Files must be in PDF format and "text-searchable" (OCR).
6. **Payment:** Fees are usually paid via "Fee Account" (PBA) for firms or via credit/debit card.
7. **Confirmation:** The system provides an immediate email receipt and a "Filing Received" status, followed by "Filing Accepted" once processed by the court office.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Electronic filing is mandatory for all legally represented parties in the Business and Property Courts." — *Practice Direction 51O*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
For a solo advocate, mastery of **CE-File** is non-negotiable for High Court work. They must ensure all their PDFs are OCR-enabled and properly bookmarked to comply with court e-bundle requirements. For debt recovery, using **MCOL/OCMC** is significantly faster and cheaper than paper filing. The soloist should maintain an HMCTS "Fee Account" (PBA) to streamline payment of court fees across all digital systems.
