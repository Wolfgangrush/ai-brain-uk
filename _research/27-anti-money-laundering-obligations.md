## Authority Tier
Statute / Regulator Guidance (Binding)

## Currency Warning
The LSAG guidance was updated in **April 2025** to reflect the **Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023** and new requirements for "Proliferation Financing" (PF) risk assessments.

## Key Facts
1. **Practice-Wide Risk Assessment (PWRA):** Every firm (including soloists) must have a written PWRA identifying the risks of ML/TF/PF relevant to their practice.
2. **Customer Due Diligence (CDD):** You must identify the client and verify their identity using "independent and reliable" sources.
3. **Beneficial Ownership:** You must identify anyone owning or controlling >25% of a corporate client.
4. **Source of Funds (SoF):** You must understand where the money for a specific transaction came from.
5. **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):** Mandatory for high-risk clients, transactions, or **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)**.
6. **Nominated Officer (MLRO):** A solo practitioner is typically their own MLRO and must register with the SRA as such.
7. **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs):** If you suspect money laundering, you must file a SAR with the National Crime Agency (NCA) and **not "tip off"** the client.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A relevant person must... establish and maintain a written record of the steps taken to identify and assess the risks of money laundering and terrorist financing." — *MLR 2017, Reg 18*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
AML is the highest-risk area for SRA enforcement. A solo advocate must have a **written AML Policy** and a **PWRA**. They should use digital ID&V tools to verify clients and must *never* accept cash payments over a small limit (e.g., £500). Documentation is key: "if it isn't written down, it didn't happen" in the eyes of the SRA. The advocate must also be vigilant about "Source of Wealth" in high-value transactions (e.g., conveyancing or corporate).
