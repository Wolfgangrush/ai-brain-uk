## Currency Warning
The CPR is updated frequently (usually twice a year) via "CPR Update" Statutory Instruments. Always check the **"Latest Update"** section on the Justice.gov.uk site. Significant recent changes include the **Fixed Recoverable Costs (FRC)** expansion in October 2023.

## Key Facts
1. **The Overriding Objective (Part 1):** The core principle that the court must deal with cases "justly and at proportionate cost." This includes saving expense and ensuring cases are dealt with expeditiously.
2. **Pre-Action Protocols:** Parties are expected to exchange information and attempt settlement *before* issuing proceedings. Failure to comply can result in costs sanctions.
3. **Case Management Tracks:** Once a claim is defended, it is allocated to one of four tracks:
    - **Small Claims Track:** Generally for claims up to £10,000.
    - **Fast Track:** For claims between £10,000 and £25,000.
    - **Intermediate Track:** For claims between £25,000 and £100,000 (introduced 2023).
    - **Multi-Track:** For complex claims or those over £100,000.
4. **Service (Part 6):** Strict rules on how and when claim forms and other documents must be served on the defendant.
5. **Standard Disclosure (Part 31):** Parties must disclose documents on which they rely and those which adversely affect their own case or support another party's case.
6. **Part 36 Offers:** A powerful tactical tool for settlement. If a party rejects an offer and fails to beat it at trial, they face significant costs and interest penalties.
7. **Fixed Recoverable Costs (FRC):** In the Fast and Intermediate tracks, the amount of costs a winning party can recover is generally fixed by the rules, limiting the financial risk but also the potential fee recovery.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"These Rules are a new procedural code with the overriding objective of enabling the court to deal with cases justly and at proportionate cost." — *CPR r.1.1(1)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
For a solo practitioner, CPR compliance is the "daily bread." Missing a limitation period or a service deadline (Part 6) is often fatal to a claim. The **Overriding Objective** means that advocates must be prepared to justify every procedural step in terms of cost and necessity. Fixed Recoverable Costs make "proportionality" a mathematical reality for solo firms—you must work within the fixed fee or risk your client being out of pocket.
