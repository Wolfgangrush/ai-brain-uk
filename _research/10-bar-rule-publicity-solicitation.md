Bar / Regulator Rule

## Currency Warning
The SRA Transparency Rules (2018) and BSB Transparency Rules (2019) introduced mandatory requirements for publishing prices and services on websites. These are strictly enforced by "mystery shopping" audits.

## Key Facts
1. **Accurate Publicity (SRA 8.8 / BSB rC19):** All publicity must be accurate and not misleading. This includes claims about expertise, success rates, and fees.
2. **Prohibition on "Cold Calling" (SRA 8.9):** Solicitors must not make unsolicited approaches to members of the public (except current or former clients) to advertise legal services. This includes in-person or telephone solicitation.
3. **Professionalism (BSB rC19):** Advertising must not diminish public trust and confidence in the profession or be incompatible with the status of a barrister.
4. **Mandatory Price Transparency:** Both solicitors and barristers must publish prices (or pricing models), service descriptions, and complaint procedures on their websites for specific areas of law (e.g., probate, employment tribunals, motoring offences).
5. **Branding and "Chambers":** Self-employed barristers must ensure their branding (e.g., a shared website for Chambers) does not mislead clients into thinking they are a single firm or partnership.
6. **Claims Management:** Strict rules apply to marketing for personal injury work; referral fees for PI cases are generally prohibited (Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"You must not make unsolicited approaches to members of the public, other than current or former clients, in order to advertise legal services provided by you, or any business or organisation in which you are at the material time a manager or has an interest." — *SRA Code of Conduct 8.9*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate must rely on "passive" marketing (websites, social media, SEO, directory listings) rather than "active" solicitation. The "Rule 36" equivalent in the UK is the ban on cold-calling. For a solo practitioner, website compliance is high-risk—failing to display mandatory price information can lead to SRA/BSB fines. Social media posts must be professional and vetted for accuracy.
