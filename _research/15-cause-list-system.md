## Currency Warning
The CaTH service on GOV.UK has largely replaced the old system of daily PDF uploads. HMCTS is moving towards a fully digital, searchable listing system as part of the "Common Platform" rollout.

## Key Facts
1. **CaTH Service:** The central government portal for "Find a court or tribunal hearing." Covers Civil, Family, Crown, Magistrates', and most Tribunals.
2. **CourtServe:** A licensed private portal that provides the most comprehensive aggregation of lists, including regional County and Magistrates' courts. It offers automated email alerts for legal professionals.
3. **XHIBIT:** Provides real-time "live" status of hearings in the Crown Court (e.g., whether a jury is out or a witness is giving evidence).
4. **Publication Time:** Lists for the following day are typically published between **4:00 PM and 5:00 PM**.
5. **RCJ & Rolls Building:** Specialized lists for the High Court (London) and Court of Appeal are published daily on GOV.UK and CourtServe.
6. **Open Justice:** Most lists are public, but "Private" or "In Camera" hearings (common in Family or sensitive Criminal matters) may be listed with initials only or excluded.
7. **Remote Access:** Listing entries often specify if a hearing is "Hybrid" or "Remote" (via CVP or MS Teams).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate must check the cause lists every evening to confirm the courtroom and start time for the following day. Since soloists often manage multiple cases, "listing alerts" (via CourtServe) are essential to prevent missing a hearing if a case is suddenly moved or listed at short notice. For Crown Court work, using XHIBIT allows the advocate to track their progress and manage their time (and client expectations) more effectively.
