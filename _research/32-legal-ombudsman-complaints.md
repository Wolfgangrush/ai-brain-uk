Statute / Regulator Rule

## Currency Warning
The Scheme Rules were significantly updated on **1 April 2023**, shortening the time limits for referring a complaint to **one year** (down from 6 years).

## Key Facts
1. **First-Tier Complaints:** A client must first complain to the law firm. The firm has **8 weeks** to resolve it.
2. **Second-Tier (LeO):** If unresolved after 8 weeks, the client can go to the Legal Ombudsman.
3. **Jurisdiction:** LeO handles "service" complaints (delays, poor communication, unclear fees). It does not handle "conduct" (which goes to SRA/BSB) or "negligence" (though they often overlap).
4. **Time Limits:**
    - **6 Months** from the firm's final response.
    - **1 Year** from the date of the act/omission.
5. **Fair and Reasonable:** LeO decides cases based on what is "fair and reasonable," not just strict law.
6. **Remedies:** LeO can order the firm to apologize, return files, or pay compensation up to **£50,000**.
7. **The "Case Fee":** Firms are charged a fee (currently £400) for every case LeO investigates, even if the firm wins (unless the firm is deemed to have handled the internal complaint perfectly).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Ombudsman will determine a complaint by reference to what is, in his or her opinion, fair and reasonable in all the circumstances of the case." — *LeO Scheme Rule 5.37*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
For a solo advocate, a LeO complaint is a major distraction and financial risk (due to the case fee). The goal must be to resolve complaints at the **First Tier**. A solo practitioner should have a simple, written complaints procedure and should respond to disgruntled clients immediately with an apology and a plan, rather than waiting for the 8-week deadline.
