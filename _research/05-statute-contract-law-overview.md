## Currency Warning
The **Consumer Rights Act 2015** largely replaced the Sale of Goods Act 1979 for consumer (B2C) contracts. SGA 1979 remains the primary law for business-to-business (B2B) and consumer-to-consumer (C2C) transactions.

## Key Facts
1. **Satisfactory Quality (CRA s.9 / SGA s.14):** Goods must meet the standard that a reasonable person would regard as satisfactory, taking into account description, price, and other relevant circumstances.
2. **Fitness for Purpose (CRA s.10 / SGA s.14):** If the buyer makes known a particular purpose for the goods, they must be fit for that purpose.
3. **Description (CRA s.11 / SGA s.13):** Goods must match any description applied to them.
4. **The "Short-term Right to Reject" (CRA s.20):** In consumer contracts, a buyer has a 30-day window to reject faulty goods and get a full refund.
5. **Repair or Replacement (CRA s.23):** After 30 days, the consumer is generally entitled to a repair or replacement.
6. **Passing of Property (SGA s.18):** Rules for determining exactly *when* ownership of goods passes from seller to buyer—crucial for risk of loss and insolvency.
7. **Exclusion Clauses:** In B2B contracts, exclusions of SGA implied terms are subject to the **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA)** "reasonableness" test. In B2C contracts, unfair terms are generally void under Part 2 of the CRA 2015.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Every contract to supply goods by description is to be treated as including a term that the goods will match the description." — *Consumer Rights Act 2015, s.11(1)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate handling breach of contract claims must first determine if the contract is B2B or B2C. For B2C, the **Consumer Rights Act 2015** provides much stronger, non-excludable protections and simple "tier 1" remedies (refund/repair). For B2B, the advocate must scrutinize the "small print" for exclusion clauses and apply the UCTA reasonableness test. Understanding "passing of property" is vital for advice on commercial disputes involving insolvency or damaged goods.
