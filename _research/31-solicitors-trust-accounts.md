# Solicitors' Accounts Rules — UK

## Source URL
- [SRA Accounts Rules 2019 (Official Site)](https://www.sra.org.uk/solicitors/standards-regulations/accounts-rules/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule

## Currency Warning
The 2019 Rules are much shorter and "principles-based" than the previous 2011 version. They emphasize "outcomes" (keeping client money safe) rather than strict mechanical steps.

## Key Facts
1. **Client Money:** Money held or received on behalf of a client or third party (including fees paid *before* a bill is delivered).
2. **Prompt Payment (Rule 2.3):** Client money must be paid **promptly** into a client account.
3. **Separation (Rule 4.1):** You must keep client money separate from the firm's own money.
4. **Withdrawal (Rule 5.1):** You can only withdraw client money to make a payment to/on behalf of the client, or to pay the firm's bill *after* it has been delivered.
5. **No Client Account? (Rule 2.2):** Solo practitioners can avoid a client account if they **only** receive money for fees/disbursements and inform the client of the risks.
6. **Accounting Records (Rule 8):** You must keep accurate, contemporaneous records showing every transaction and the current balance for each client.
7. **Reconciliations (Rule 8.3):** You must reconcile your client account at least **every five weeks**.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"You keep client money separate from money belonging to the authorised body or yourself." — *SRA Accounts Rules, Rule 4.1*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
The SRA Accounts Rules are "strict liability." A solo practitioner who "borrows" client money (even for a day) faces certain strike-off. Many soloists use **Third-Party Managed Accounts (TPMA)** or strictly bill in arrears to avoid the massive administrative burden and audit costs of maintaining a traditional client account. The "5-week reconciliation" is a mandatory diary date for any solo firm.
