## Currency Warning
The consolidated version on Justice.gov.uk is the most reliable. The rules are frequently amended (e.g., by the Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Rules). Ensure you are looking at the version that includes the most recent **Criminal Practice Directions**.

## Key Facts
1. **The Overriding Objective (Part 1):** To deal with criminal cases "justly." This includes acquitting the innocent and convicting the guilty, dealing with the prosecution and defense fairly, and respecting the defendant's Article 6 (ECHR) rights.
2. **Case Management (Part 3):** The court must take an active role in managing cases. Parties must assist the court by identifying issues early and adhering to timetables.
3. **Service of Documents (Part 4):** Rules for serving documents on the court and other parties, including electronic service.
4. **Initial Details of the Prosecution Case (Part 8):** The prosecution must provide the defendant with sufficient information (e.g., summary of facts, witness statements) to allow for an informed plea at the first hearing.
5. **Disclosure (Part 15):** Governed primarily by the **Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA)**, but CrimPR sets the procedural framework for requesting and managing disclosure of unused material.
6. **Witness Statements (Part 16) and Exhibits:** Specific formats required for written evidence to be admissible without the witness attending (if agreed).
7. **Hearsay and Bad Character (Parts 20 & 21):** Procedural rules for making applications to introduce hearsay or bad character evidence under the Criminal Justice Act 2003.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The overriding objective of this procedural code is that criminal cases be dealt with justly." — *CrimPR r.1.1(1)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
In criminal defense, a solo advocate's primary duty is early identification of the real issues. The **Overriding Objective** requires the defense to tell the court *what is in dispute* at the first hearing (the "Effective Trial Preparation" form). Failure to engage with case management can lead to the court refusing to allow late evidence or witnesses. Mastery of Part 8 (Initial Details) is crucial for advising on plea at the Magistrates' Court stage.
