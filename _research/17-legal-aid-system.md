## Currency Warning
The system was drastically cut by **LASPO 2012**. However, recent "legal aid deserts" have led to the **Criminal Legal Aid Review (CLAR)** and increases in fees for some areas (2024/25). The 2026 means-test thresholds have also been slightly adjusted for inflation.

## Key Facts
1. **Legal Aid Agency (LAA):** Administers public funding for legal services in England and Wales.
2. **Criminal Legal Aid:**
    - **Interests of Justice (IoJ) Test:** Is it "right" to fund this (e.g., risk of prison)?
    - **Means Test:** Financial eligibility based on disposable income (Threshold: £3,398 disposable income in Magistrates' Court; £37,500 in Crown Court).
3. **Civil Legal Aid:**
    - **In-Scope:** Only specific cases (e.g., domestic abuse, homelessness, asylum, SEN).
    - **Merits Test:** Is the case likely to succeed and reasonable to fund?
    - **Means Test:** Strict income (<£2,657 gross/month) and capital (<£8,000) limits.
4. **Exceptional Case Funding (ECF):** Funding for "out-of-scope" cases where human rights would be breached otherwise.
5. **Duty Solicitor Scheme:** Provides free advice at police stations and Magistrates' Courts regardless of means.
6. **Statutory Charge:** If a client wins money or property in a civil case, the LAA may take a "charge" from the proceeds to repay the legal aid costs.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Legal aid is available for the purposes of a criminal case... if the individual is an eligible individual." — *LASPO 2012, s.13*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
For solo advocates in Criminal or Family law, the LAA is their "primary client." They must be "LAA Authorized" to take these cases. The administrative burden of "claiming" (using the LAA portal) is significant. The **Statutory Charge** is a major "trap" for clients—advocates must warn them that their "win" might be entirely consumed by the LAA's bill.
