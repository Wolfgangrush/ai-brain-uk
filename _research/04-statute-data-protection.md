## Currency Warning
The **Data (Use and Access) Act 2025** (DUAA) received Royal Assent recently (June 2025) and is now amending parts of the DPA 2018 and UK GDPR. Practitioners must monitor the phased commencement of DUAA provisions. Note that "UK GDPR" is the retained EU GDPR as modified by UK law post-Brexit.

## Key Facts
1. **Six Data Protection Principles:** Personal data must be:
    - Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
    - Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes.
    - Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary.
    - Accurate and kept up to date.
    - Kept in a form that permits identification for no longer than necessary.
    - Processed securely (integrity and confidentiality).
2. **The Accountability Principle:** Organizations must not only comply but be able to *demonstrate* compliance (e.g., through records of processing, DPIAs).
3. **Lawful Bases for Processing:** You must have a valid legal basis (e.g., Consent, Contract, Legal Obligation, Vital Interests, Public Task, or Legitimate Interests).
4. **Data Subject Rights:** Individuals have rights to access (SARs), rectify, erase ("right to be forgotten"), restrict processing, and data portability.
5. **Special Category Data:** Stricter rules for sensitive data (race, religion, health, sexual orientation, etc.). Requires an additional condition from Article 9 UK GDPR or Schedule 1 DPA 2018.
6. **Breach Notification:** Personal data breaches that pose a risk to individuals must be reported to the ICO within 72 hours.
7. **Enforcement:** The ICO has the power to issue warnings, reprimands, and significant fines (up to £17.5m or 4% of global turnover).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, paragraph 1 (‘accountability’)." — *UK GDPR Article 5(2)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates are "data controllers" of their clients' sensitive personal information. They must:
- Register with the ICO and pay the data protection fee.
- Provide a clear **Privacy Notice** to clients.
- Have a robust system for handling **Subject Access Requests (SARs)**—a common tactic in litigation.
- Ensure cybersecurity (encryption, secure cloud) to prevent breaches of legal professional privilege and data protection law.
- Be aware of the **Law Enforcement Processing** rules (Part 3 DPA 2018) if handling criminal defense cases.
