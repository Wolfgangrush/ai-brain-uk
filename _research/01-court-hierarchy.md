## Currency Warning
The system was significantly restructured by the **Constitutional Reform Act 2005** (which created the Supreme Court) and the **Crime and Courts Act 2013** (which created the single Family Court and single County Court).

## Key Facts
1. **The Supreme Court:** The final court of appeal for all UK civil cases and criminal cases from England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. It hears appeals on "arguable points of law of general public importance."
2. **Court of Appeal:** Divided into Criminal and Civil divisions. Hears appeals from the High Court, Crown Court, and certain Tribunals.
3. **High Court:** Superior court of first instance for complex cases. Divided into three divisions:
    - **King’s Bench Division:** Contract, tort, and the Administrative Court (Judicial Review).
    - **Chancery Division:** Equity, trusts, tax, intellectual property, and bankruptcy.
    - **Family Division:** Complex matrimonial and children's cases.
4. **Crown Court:** Hears serious criminal cases (indictable-only) and appeals from Magistrates' Courts. Trials involve a judge and jury.
5. **County Court:** A single national court (since 2014) dealing with the majority of civil claims, including debt recovery, personal injury, and housing disputes.
6. **Magistrates' Court:** Where virtually all criminal cases start. Handles "summary" offences and some limited civil matters (e.g., council tax).
7. **Family Court:** A unified court for most family law matters, utilizing various levels of judges.
8. **Tribunal System:** Specialized system (First-tier and Upper Tribunal) for specific areas like tax, immigration, and employment. The Upper Tribunal is equivalent in status to the High Court.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Senior Courts of England and Wales shall consist of the Court of Appeal, the High Court of Justice and the Crown Court." — *Senior Courts Act 1981, s.1(1)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate must correctly identify the "gateway" court. Most civil debt or contract work for a solo practitioner will start in the **County Court** via the Money Claim Online (MCOL) system or the Civil National Business Centre. Criminal defense work always begins in the **Magistrates' Court**. Understanding the hierarchy is critical for determining the route of appeal and the binding nature of precedents (decisions of higher courts bind lower courts).
