## Currency Warning
The UK government is currently (2026) considering a "targeted" AI Bill following the "AI Regulation: a pro-innovation approach" white paper. The **EU AI Act** has extraterritorial reach and already impacts UK firms serving EU clients.

## Key Facts
1. **Human-in-the-Loop:** Both the SRA and BSB mandate that AI cannot replace the professional judgment of a lawyer. The lawyer remains personally accountable for the accuracy of all AI-generated work (including citations).
2. **Confidentiality Risk:** Inputting privileged client data into public LLMs (like the free version of ChatGPT) is a likely breach of SRA Rule 6.3 and BSB CD6.
3. **Hallucinations:** High Court cases (e.g., *Harber v Commissioners for HMRC [2023]*) have established that citing AI-hallucinated cases is a serious professional failure.
4. **EU AI Act "High-Risk":** AI used in the "administration of justice" is classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act. UK firms using such tools for EU-based clients must comply with strict data quality and human oversight rules.
5. **AI Firm Authorization:** The SRA has begun authorizing "AI-first" firms (e.g., for standardized immigration tasks), but these firms must have a "qualified solicitor" in a compliance role.
6. **Transparency:** Lawyers should consider disclosing to clients if AI is being used significantly in their matter, especially if it affects the billing structure.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The solicitor is responsible for the work they produce. This responsibility cannot be delegated to an AI tool." — *SRA Guidance (Paraphrased)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate using AI (e.g., for drafting or research) must use **Enterprise/Private instances** of LLMs that guarantee data privacy (no training on user data). They must **check every citation** manually. The soloist should update their **Terms of Business** to specify that AI tools may be used to assist in the matter but that the final work is vetted by the advocate. For those doing EU-related work, a compliance audit against the EU AI Act is necessary to avoid significant fines.
