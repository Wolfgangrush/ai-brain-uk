# Citation Format (Secondary Sources) — UK (OSCOLA)

## Source URL
- [OSCOLA 4th Edition (Oxford Law)](https://www.law.ox.ac.uk/oscola)

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Reputable Secondary (Industry Standard)

## Currency Warning
OSCOLA 4th Edition is the current standard. Ensure you use the correct abbreviations for journals (e.g., *Law Quarterly Review* is `LQR`).

## Key Facts
1. **Books:**
    - *Format:* Author, *Title* (Edition, Publisher Year) Pinpoint.
    - *Example:* Timothy Endicott, *Administrative Law* (4th edn, OUP 2018) 45.
2. **Journal Articles:**
    - *Format:* Author, 'Title' [Year] Journal Abbreviation First Page.
    - *Example:* Paul Craig, 'Theory, "Pure Theory" and Values in Public Law' [2005] PL 440.
    - *Note:* Use round brackets (Year) if there is a separate volume number.
3. **Subsequent Citations:**
    - **Immediate:** Use `ibid`.
    - **Later:** Use Surname (n [first footnote number]) [pinpoint].
    - *Example:* Endicott (n 1) 50.
4. **Minimal Punctuation:** No full stops in abbreviations or at the end of titles.
5. **Bibliography:**
    - Secondary sources are listed alphabetically by author surname.
    - Format: Surname Initial, *Title* (Edition, Publisher Year).
    - *Example:* Endicott T, *Administrative Law* (4th edn, OUP 2018)
6. **Websites:**
    - *Format:* Author, 'Title' (Website Name, Date) <URL> accessed Date.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Cite secondary sources in the same way as primary sources: concisely and accurately." — *OSCOLA 4th Ed (Paraphrased)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
When drafting skeleton arguments, using OSCOLA for secondary sources (like *Archbold* or *Chitty on Contracts*) is expected in the High Court and Court of Appeal. A solo advocate should maintain a "style sheet" for their common references to ensure consistency and professional presentation.
