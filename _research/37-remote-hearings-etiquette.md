## Currency Warning
The **Video Hearings Service (VHS)** is the current strategic platform, having replaced the old Cloud Video Platform (CVP) for most matters in 2025/26. MS Teams is now rarely used for formal hearings.

## Key Facts
1. **Legal Effect:** A remote hearing has the same legal status and effect as an in-person hearing.
2. **Dress Code:** Advocates must wear professional business attire (suit/gown as appropriate) regardless of their location.
3. **Recording Offence:** It is a **criminal offence** (s.85A Courts Act 2003) to record, broadcast, or take screen-captures of a remote hearing.
4. **Environment:** Advocates must be in a private, secure room with a neutral background and frontal lighting. No other person should be present unless authorized.
5. **VHS Features:** The VHS platform includes virtual consultation rooms for advocates to speak privately with clients during "breaks."
6. **E-Bundles:** Bundles for remote hearings must be "indexed and paginated" in a single PDF, fully OCR-searchable, and bookmarked.
7. **Public Access (PD 51ZH):** Since 2026, advocates in certain courts must file "Public Domain Documents" (skeletons, reports) to CE-File within 48 hours of a public hearing to ensure transparency.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The conduct of a remote hearing must be as close as possible to the conduct of a hearing in a courtroom." — *Judicial College Guidance (Paraphrased)*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate's "office" is now also their "courtroom." They must invest in high-quality lighting and a professional-grade microphone/headset to ensure they are heard clearly. Mastery of the **VHS platform** (including screen sharing for evidence) is a core advocacy skill. Soloists must also be expert in **E-bundle creation**, as a poorly formatted PDF can irritate a judge and delay a remote hearing.
