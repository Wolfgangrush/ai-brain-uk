## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule

## Currency Warning
Both regulators have moved from "hour-counting" to a **Reflective Model**. There is no longer a mandatory "16-hour" rule for solicitors or "established" barristers.

## Key Facts
1. **SRA Continuing Competence:** Solicitors must ensure they are competent to perform their role. This involves a four-stage cycle: **Reflect, Plan, Act, Evaluate**.
2. **Annual Declaration:** Every solicitor must declare annually (during the October renewal) that they have reflected on their practice and addressed their learning needs.
3. **BSB Established Practitioner Programme (EPP):** Barristers with >3 years practice must follow a similar reflective cycle (Plan, Act, Record, Reflect).
4. **BSB New Practitioner Programme (NPP):** Barristers in their first 3 years must complete **45 hours** of CPD, including 9h of advocacy and 3h of ethics.
5. **Record Keeping:** You must maintain a record of your reflections and training (e.g., a "Learning Log"). The SRA and BSB conduct "spot checks" of these logs.
6. **Mandatory Training:** While the *topics* are self-selected, lawyers are effectively mandated to stay updated on high-risk regulatory areas like AML, GDPR, and the SRA/BSB Codes of Conduct.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"You must maintain a proper standard of service and keep your professional knowledge and skills up to date." — *SRA Code of Conduct, Para 3.1*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
For a solo advocate, "continuing competence" is about more than just law; it includes **Practice Management** and **Technology**. The soloist's learning log should reflect their efforts to master new court systems (e.g., CE-File) and regulatory changes (e.g., new AML guidance). A solo practitioner who fails to keep a log risks their practicing certificate if they are audited by the SRA/BSB.
