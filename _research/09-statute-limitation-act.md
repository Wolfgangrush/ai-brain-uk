# Limitation Act 1980 — UK

## Source URL
- [Limitation Act 1980 (legislation.gov.uk)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1980/58/contents)

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute

## Currency Warning
The Act is stable but has been amended by subsequent legislation (e.g., for latent damage and defamation). Always check for specific "special" limitation periods in other acts (e.g., the Carriage of Goods by Sea Act).

## Key Facts
1. **Simple Contract (s.5):** 6 years from the date the cause of action accrued (usually the date of the breach).
2. **Tort (s.2):** 6 years from the date the cause of action accrued (when the damage occurred).
3. **Personal Injury (s.11):** 3 years from the date of accrual or the "date of knowledge" of the injured person (whichever is later).
4. **Actions on a Deed (s.8):** 12 years from the date of accrual.
5. **Recovery of Land (s.15):** 12 years.
6. **Defamation / Malicious Falsehood (s.4A):** 1 year from the date of publication.
7. **Latent Damage (s.14A):** In negligence claims (not PI), 3 years from the "date of knowledge," subject to a 15-year "long-stop" (s.14B).
8. **Disability (s.28):** The clock does not start if the claimant is a minor or lacks mental capacity until the disability ceases or they die.
9. **Fraud, Concealment, or Mistake (s.32):** The period does not begin until the claimant has discovered the fraud/concealment/mistake or could with "reasonable diligence" have discovered it.
10. **Section 33 Discretion:** In PI and death claims, the court has broad discretion to "disapply" the limitation period if equitable.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"An action founded on simple contract shall not be brought after the expiration of six years from the date on which the cause of action accrued." — *Limitation Act 1980, s.5*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A limitation period is a "hard" deadline. Missing it is professional negligence. A solo advocate must:
- Record the limitation date immediately upon taking instructions.
- Be extremely cautious with the 1-year defamation limit.
- Understand that "accrual" in contract happens at the breach, even if the client doesn't know about it yet.
- Use **Standstill Agreements** with the defendant's consent to pause the clock during negotiations, or issue "protective proceedings" if the deadline is near.
