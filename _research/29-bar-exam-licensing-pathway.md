## Currency Warning
The **SQE** replaced the old LPC in 2021. The 2026 updates include reduced questions in SQE1 and "hybrid" advocacy in SQE2. The BSB recently (2025) removed the rigid 2:2 degree requirement for Bar course entry.

## Key Facts
1. **Solicitor Pathway (SQE):**
    - **Degree:** Any degree or equivalent.
    - **SQE1:** Two exams on Functioning Legal Knowledge (FLK1 & FLK2).
    - **SQE2:** Assessment of practical legal skills (Oral and Written).
    - **Qualifying Work Experience (QWE):** 2 years of work in legal services (can be split across 4 organizations).
    - **Character & Suitability:** Final SRA check.
2. **Barrister Pathway:**
    - **Academic Stage:** Law degree or GDL (2:2 requirement now flexible).
    - **Vocational Stage:** Bar Practice Course (BPC) or similar. Includes centralized BSB exams in Ethics and Litigation.
    - **Inns of Court:** Must join one of the four Inns and complete "Qualifying Sessions."
    - **Pupillage:** 12 months of work-based learning (6 months "non-practising," 6 months "practising").
3. **Cross-Qualification:** Qualified lawyers from other jurisdictions (e.g., India, USA) can often skip QWE and may be exempt from parts of the SQE.
4. **Fees (2026):** SQE exams total ~£5,000. Bar courses range from £12,000 to £20,000.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"To be admitted as a solicitor, you must pass the SQE, have a degree, complete two years QWE, and meet our character and suitability requirements." — *SRA Guidance*

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
The SQE has made it easier for experienced paralegals or foreign lawyers to become solo advocates by counting their existing experience as **QWE**. However, a solo practitioner must still have **3 years PQE** to handle "reserved activities" (litigation, probate) on their own account as a Freelance Solicitor. For barristers, the competitive "Pupillage" stage remains the biggest barrier to entry for solo practice.
