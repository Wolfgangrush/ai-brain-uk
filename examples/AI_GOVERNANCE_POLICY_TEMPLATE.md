## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy governs the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools by [Chambers/Practice Name] ("the Chambers") in the delivery of legal services. It applies to all barristers, pupils, clerks, practice managers, and administrative staff.

This policy is designed to ensure compliance with:
- The BSB Handbook (as amended), including Core Duties CD1-CD10
- The BSB Artificial Intelligence Guidance (effective 18 May 2026)
- UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
- The Equality Act 2010

---

## 2. Acceptable Uses of AI

| Risk Level | Application | Permitted Tools | Requires |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Administrative tasks (calendar, time recording, formatting) | Any | General awareness |
| **Medium** | Legal research, document summarisation, general client advice preparation | Legal-specific AI in secure environment | Supervisor approval + rC19 disclosure |
| **High** | Court submissions, drafting pleadings, automated/agentic AI, vulnerable clients, protected characteristics matters | Chambers-approved tools only, local-first architecture | Head of Chambers approval + rC19 written disclosure + independent verification + audit log |

### Prohibited Uses
- Entering client-confidential data into free-of-charge, publicly available AI systems (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude.ai free tier, Google Gemini free tier)
- Using AI-generated content in court submissions without independent human verification
- Delegating professional judgment to AI (rC20)
- Using AI in matters involving protected characteristics without bias-review

---

## 3. Record Keeping (rC87.2)

The Chambers shall maintain an AI Audit Log recording:
- Date and time of each AI use
- The AI tool used
- The nature of the task (application, use, and technology axes per BSB guidance)
- Whether the output was verified by counsel
- Whether rC19 client disclosure was made

Audit logs shall be retained for a minimum of **90 days** and stored locally within Chambers' systems. Logs shall not be transmitted to third parties.

---

## 4. Data Governance (rC15 + UK GDPR)

- **Local-first architecture:** AI tools used for client-confidential work shall operate locally within Chambers' systems. Data shall not be transmitted to cloud AI providers except where an Article 28 Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and Schrems-II supplementary measures are in place.
- **LPP preservation:** Legal Professional Privilege is maintained only where AI processing occurs within Chambers' controlled environment. See Munir [2026] UKUT 81 (IAC).
- **ICO compliance:** AI processing of personal data must be documented in the Chambers' Records of Processing Activities (ROPA) per Article 30 UK GDPR. Data subjects must be informed of AI processing per Article 5(1)(a) UK GDPR. See Oakley v Information Commissioner [2024] UKFTT 315 (GRC).
- **ICO LOCS alignment:** This policy aligns with the ICO's guidance on lawful basis, transparency, data minimisation, and storage limitation for AI systems.

## 5. AI Governance Procedures

### 5.1 Before Adoption (BSB Part C)
- Conduct a risk assessment using the BSB's 3×3 matrix (application × use × technology)
- Evaluate AI tools against rC86.3 conditions: (a) no compromise of Core Duties, (b) client consent where required, (c) adequate confidentiality safeguards, (d) ability to override/monitor
- Document the assessment and obtain Head of Chambers / COLP approval

### 5.2 Setting Procedures (BSB Part D)
- Establish standard operating procedures for AI use
- Appoint an AI Compliance Lead
- Train all Chambers members on AI policy and risks
- Configure audit logging before any AI tool is deployed

### 5.3 Each Use (BSB Part E)
- Verify AI output independently before reliance (rC20)
- Flag AI-generated citations for 2-source verification (rC9.1/rC9.2)
- Disclose AI use to clients per rC19
- Log the use in the audit trail

### 5.4 Others Using AI (BSB Part F)
- Pupils and junior tenants must have AI outputs reviewed by a supervising barrister
- External solicitors' AI-generated materials must be independently verified
- Clerks using AI for administrative tasks must follow Chambers' data handling procedures

---

## 6. Prompt Deletion Policy

- AI prompts containing client-confidential information shall not be retained by cloud AI providers where deletion mechanisms exist
- Chambers shall maintain a prompt deletion schedule, verified quarterly
- Where cloud AI use is unavoidable, prompts shall be pseudonymised before submission

---

## 7. Client Disclosure Template (rC19)

The following shall be included in client care letters / engagement letters where AI materially impacts service delivery:

> "In accordance with BSB rC19, we confirm that artificial intelligence tools may be used to assist in the preparation of your case. All AI-assisted work is independently reviewed by your barrister. No client-confidential data is transmitted to third-party AI providers. Our AI tools operate locally within Chambers' secure environment. You have the right to request further information about how AI is used in your matter."

---

## 8. Breach Reporting

Any suspected breach of this policy shall be reported to the AI Compliance Lead within 24 hours. Material breaches affecting client confidentiality or court submissions shall be reported to the BSB and ICO as required.
